## Background

James Thornton is a Solutions Engineer at CyberLLM, supporting pre-sales technical
demonstrations, proof-of-concept deployments, and technical evaluations for all eight CyberLLM
products. He serves as the technical bridge between the sales team and engineering, translating
customer security requirements into tailored product configurations and ensuring prospective
customers see CyberLLM's value in their own environment. Before joining CyberLLM, he spent
five years as a Solutions Architect at FireEye (now Trellix), where he supported enterprise
sales cycles for FireEye's network security, endpoint, and email security products. He led
over 150 proof-of-concept deployments and maintained a 72% POC-to-close conversion rate — one
of the highest on FireEye's global SE team.

Prior to FireEye, James worked as a security engineer at a Dallas-based financial services firm
for three years, managing Splunk SIEM deployments, firewall rule administration, and incident
response. This operational security background gives him credibility with technical evaluators
during sales cycles. He holds a BS in Information Technology from the University of Texas at
Dallas, is a Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP), and maintains a
CompTIA Security+ certification. He is also certified in AWS Solutions Architecture.

James was recruited by Sarah Mitchell after she encountered him during CyberLLM's competitive
evaluation against FireEye at a Fortune 500 healthcare company. Impressed by his technical
depth and demo skills, she convinced Marcus Chen to create a dedicated SE role. At CyberLLM,
James has supported over 80 enterprise sales cycles, built a reusable demo environment library
covering all products, and created the "CyberLLM Technical Validation Guide" used by prospects
during evaluations. He works closely with Liam O'Sullivan on automated POC environment
provisioning and with every product manager on demo roadmap alignment.

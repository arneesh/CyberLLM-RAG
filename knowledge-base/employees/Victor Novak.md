## Background

Victor Novak is a QA Engineer at CyberLLM, responsible for designing and maintaining the
automated testing frameworks that ensure product quality across the company's security platform.
Before CyberLLM, he spent five years at Fortinet as a QA Lead on the FortiEDR and FortiSIEM
product lines, where he managed a team of four QA engineers and built the automated regression
testing suite that covered 2,400+ test cases across endpoint detection, network monitoring, and
threat prevention features. His work at Fortinet reduced release-blocking defects by 70% over
three years and cut regression testing cycles from five days to eight hours through parallel test
execution and intelligent test selection.

Victor earned his BS in Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign,
where he developed an interest in software quality after a class project involving formal
verification methods. Before Fortinet, he worked as a software tester at a healthcare SaaS
startup for two years, gaining experience with HIPAA compliance testing and regulated environment
QA processes. He holds an ISTQB Advanced Level Test Automation Engineer certification and is
proficient in Python, Selenium, Cypress, Playwright, and API testing frameworks (Postman,
pytest, and REST Assured).

Victor learned about CyberLLM through a former Fortinet colleague and was attracted by the
opportunity to build QA processes from the ground up at an AI-first cybersecurity startup. At
CyberLLM, he owns the automated testing frameworks for ShieldLLM (endpoint detection and
response) and NetLLM (SIEM), the two products with the most complex testing requirements due to
their real-time data processing, high-volume event correlation, and low false-positive tolerance.
He also contributes to cross-product integration testing and maintains CyberLLM's CI/CD test
pipeline, ensuring every code merge passes automated regression, performance, and security tests
before deployment.

## Background

Sarah Mitchell joined CyberLLM as VP of Sales in February 2020, tasked with building the
company's go-to-market strategy and enterprise sales engine from scratch. Before CyberLLM,
she spent seven years at Splunk, rising from regional account executive to VP of Enterprise
Sales for the East Coast. During her final two years, her team generated $120M in annual
recurring revenue across 180+ enterprise accounts, and she personally closed Splunk's largest
single deal at the time — a $14M multi-year contract with a major U.S. bank covering Splunk
Enterprise Security and SOAR. She was a three-time President's Club winner at Splunk.

Prior to Splunk, Sarah worked in enterprise sales at IBM Security (3 years) and RSA (2 years),
building a broad network across the Fortune 500 CISO community. She earned her BA in Economics
from Georgetown University and her MBA from Columbia Business School, where she specialized in
technology marketing and was a member of the Lang Center for Entrepreneurship. Her career has
been defined by her ability to build consultative sales processes for complex technical products,
translating engineering capabilities into business value propositions for C-suite buyers.

Sarah was recruited by Marcus Chen after a conversation at a Gartner Security & Risk Management
Summit in 2019. Despite joining weeks before the COVID-19 pandemic upended traditional enterprise
sales, she adapted quickly by pioneering CyberLLM's virtual demo environment ("CyberLLM Live")
and a digital-first sales playbook that has since become an industry-referenced case study. She
built the sales team from zero to eight account executives and BDRs, established a partnership
channel with four resellers, and personally closed CyberLLM's first $1M+ deal in Q4 2020. Her
strategic focus on financial services and healthcare — sectors with high regulatory pressure and
cybersecurity maturity — has been instrumental in the company's revenue trajectory.

## Background

Priya Raghavan leads CyberLLM's threat research team, overseeing the development of threat
intelligence models, APT tracking methodologies, and the research pipeline that feeds ThreatLLM's
continuously updated threat knowledge base. Before joining CyberLLM, she spent seven years at
the National Security Agency (NSA), where she served as a senior cyber threat analyst in the
Tailored Access Operations (TAO) group. Her work at the NSA focused on attributing advanced
persistent threat (APT) campaigns to nation-state actors, with particular expertise in campaigns
originating from Russia, China, North Korea, and Iran. She contributed to multiple classified
national intelligence estimates and inter-agency coordination efforts with the FBI, CIA, and CISA.

During her NSA tenure, Priya developed novel attribution frameworks combining network forensics,
malware reverse engineering, and behavioral analysis that could link seemingly disparate intrusion
campaigns to common threat actors. Her unclassified research has been published in the Journal
of Cybersecurity, IEEE Security & Privacy, and USENIX Security proceedings — a total of 9
peer-reviewed publications cited over 400 times. She holds a BS in Computer Science from Georgia
Tech (summa cum laude) and an MS in Cybersecurity from Johns Hopkins University, where her thesis
on automated malware family classification using graph neural networks won the Outstanding Thesis
award.

Priya was recruited through CyberLLM's partnership with UT Austin, where she had been a visiting
researcher after leaving the NSA in early 2019. She was drawn to the challenge of training LLMs
on threat intelligence data. At CyberLLM, she manages a team of 4 researchers and analysts
(including Alex Petrov), curates threat intelligence datasets for ThreatLLM's training pipeline,
and serves as the company's public-facing thought leader on threat landscape trends. Her team's
research has been cited in three CISA advisories and featured by Wired, KrebsOnSecurity, and
The Record.

  Built initial APT knowledge base with 200+ actor profiles. Recruited first two analysts.
  Contributed threat behavioral models to ThreatLLM v1.5. Established intelligence sharing

  Presented at Black Hat Arsenal on open-source attribution tooling.

  Knowledge base reached 25,000+ IOCs and 700+ profiles. Established adversary emulation
  program for Carlos Mendez's PenLLM team.

  team experienced burnout from increased product and customer demands. Advocated for headcount
  deferred by budget constraints. One analyst took 3-month medical leave. Priya managed workload

- **2024:** Rating: Exceeds Expectations. Hired replacement analyst and restructured workflows
  with clearer research/product boundaries. Delivered threat briefings for Fortune 100
  onboardings. Launched "CyberLLM Threat Briefs" weekly newsletter (1,500 subscribers). Co-led
  research-to-product working group with Ryan Nakamura. Team morale recovered.

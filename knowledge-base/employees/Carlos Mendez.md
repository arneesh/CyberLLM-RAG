## Background

Carlos Mendez is CyberLLM's Senior Penetration Tester and the primary architect of PenLLM's
automated penetration testing and vulnerability management modules. Before CyberLLM, he spent
four years as a red team lead at Deloitte's Cyber Risk Advisory practice in Austin, leading
offensive security engagements for Fortune 500 clients in financial services, energy, and
healthcare. His engagements consistently uncovered critical vulnerabilities including Active
Directory misconfigurations, cloud IAM privilege escalation paths, and application-layer flaws.
He developed Deloitte's internal reconnaissance toolkit ("RedAuto"), reducing attack surface
mapping time by 60% across the firm's global 200+ consultant red team practice.

Prior to Deloitte, Carlos worked as a penetration tester at Rapid7 for two years, contributing
to the Metasploit open-source project — authoring 8 exploit modules and 12 auxiliary modules
that remain in the framework's active codebase. He also developed scanning plugins for Rapid7's
Nexpose product. He holds a BS in Computer Science from the University of Texas at San Antonio
(UTSA), an NSA/DHS Center of Academic Excellence in Cyber Operations. Carlos holds OSCP, OSCE,
and GXPN certifications. His CTF team "AustinPwn" has placed top 20 at DEF CON CTF three times
(2017, 2019, 2022) and won BSides Austin CTF in 2018 and 2021.

Carlos was recruited by Elena Vasquez after his BSides Austin talk on "AI-Assisted Vulnerability
Discovery: Automating the Attacker's Playbook." At CyberLLM, he designs PenLLM's attack
simulation modules, vulnerability scoring algorithms, and automated exploit chain analysis. He
works with Priya Raghavan's threat research team to ensure simulations reflect real-world TTPs
mapped to MITRE ATT&CK. He also runs quarterly red team exercises against CyberLLM's own
infrastructure, collaborating with David Okonkwo and Jordan Williams to test defensive
capabilities in realistic adversary scenarios.

## Background

Mei-Lin Zhang is a deeply technical security engineer whose expertise in cloud security and
zero-trust architecture has made her the driving force behind CyberLLM's IdentLLM product.
She earned a B.S. in Computer Science from UC San Diego and an M.S. in Computer Science with
a security specialization from Stanford University. Her master's thesis on behavioral biometrics
for continuous authentication was published at IEEE S&P and received the Distinguished Paper
Award, establishing her reputation in the identity and access management research community
early in her career.

After Stanford, Mei-Lin joined Google as a security engineer on the BeyondCorp team — Google's
pioneering zero-trust access framework that fundamentally redefined enterprise network security.
Over five years she progressed from L4 to L6 (staff-equivalent) and contributed to core
components of BeyondCorp's device trust engine, access proxy, and policy enforcement layer.
She led the design of a machine learning-based risk scoring system that evaluated access
requests in real-time based on user behavior, device posture, and network context, processing
over 500 million access decisions per day. She also spent a year on Google's Threat Analysis
Group (TAG), investigating state-sponsored phishing campaigns targeting Google users, which
gave her valuable offensive threat intelligence experience.

Ryan Nakamura recruited Mei-Lin in 2020 through the Stanford alumni network. She was drawn to
the challenge of building a zero-trust product from scratch rather than maintaining an existing
system at Google's massive scale. As Senior Security Engineer, she is the technical lead and
core architect of IdentLLM, CyberLLM's identity and access management product that applies AI
to continuous authentication, adaptive access control, and identity threat detection. She also
contributes to VaultLLM's data classification engine and serves as the engineering team's
resident expert on cloud security across AWS, GCP, and Azure.

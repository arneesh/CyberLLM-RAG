## Background

Ryan Nakamura joined CyberLLM as VP of Engineering in March 2019, bringing nearly two decades
of experience building and scaling security product engineering teams. Before CyberLLM, he served
as Engineering Director at CrowdStrike for four years, where he managed a 60-person engineering
organization responsible for the Falcon endpoint protection platform. Under his leadership,
CrowdStrike's detection engine improved its mean time to detection by 75%, and his teams shipped
three major platform releases on schedule while maintaining 99.99% uptime across the Falcon SaaS
infrastructure. He was instrumental in CrowdStrike's engineering scaling during its pre-IPO phase.

Prior to CrowdStrike, Ryan held senior engineering roles at Symantec (5 years) and FireEye
(3 years), giving him deep exposure to the full spectrum of cybersecurity product development —
from legacy signature-based antivirus to modern behavioral analysis platforms and cloud-native
SIEM. He earned his BS in Computer Engineering from UC Berkeley and his MS in Software Engineering
from Carnegie Mellon, where his capstone project on distributed systems reliability won the
departmental award. He is known in the industry for his "engineering excellence" framework, a
methodology emphasizing TDD, continuous deployment, cross-functional collaboration, and
data-driven retrospectives.

Marcus Chen recruited Ryan after meeting him at a CrowdStrike partner event in San Francisco.
Ryan was drawn to CyberLLM's mission of applying LLMs to cybersecurity and the opportunity to
build engineering culture from the ground up. He oversees all product engineering teams across
CyberLLM's eight products, manages the San Francisco engineering hub (5 engineers), and serves
as the primary interface between engineering and sales for technical deal support. His systematic
approach to engineering management has been credited with CyberLLM's ability to ship eight
products in four years without sacrificing code quality or team morale.

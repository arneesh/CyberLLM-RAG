## Background

Jordan Williams leads CyberLLM's internal security operations center (SOC) and serves as a key
contributor to IncidentLLM product development. Before joining CyberLLM, Jordan spent three years
as a SOC analyst at Mandiant (formerly FireEye Services), working on incident response engagements
for Fortune 500 clients across financial services, healthcare, and technology. At Mandiant, Jordan
handled over 150 security incidents ranging from ransomware (Ryuk, REvil, Conti) to nation-state
APT intrusions. They developed a reputation for calm, methodical triage under pressure and were
promoted to senior analyst within 18 months — one of the fastest promotions in their office.

Prior to Mandiant, Jordan served as a junior security analyst at SecureOps, a New York-area MSSP,
monitoring SIEM alerts and performing initial triage for 40+ SMB clients. This gave them a strong
foundation in high-volume alert management, SIEM tuning, and security monitoring at scale. They
hold a BS in Cybersecurity from NYU Tandon School of Engineering, where they were a member of the
CSAW competition team and placed top 10 nationally in digital forensics (2014). Jordan holds
CompTIA Security+, CySA+, and GIAC Certified Enterprise Defender (GCED) certifications and is
currently pursuing CISSP.

Jordan was recruited by David Okonkwo, who had worked with Mandiant on a JPMorgan incident
response engagement in 2018 and was specifically impressed by Jordan's ability to correlate
disparate alert data into coherent incident narratives. At CyberLLM, Jordan manages a 3-person
SOC monitoring the company's infrastructure 24/7 using the full CyberLLM product suite. Beyond
internal operations, Jordan translates real-world SOC workflows into product requirements for
IncidentLLM. Their operational feedback has directly shaped the product's automated playbook
system, alert enrichment, analyst dashboards, and the natural-language incident summarization
feature that customers rate as IncidentLLM's most valuable capability.

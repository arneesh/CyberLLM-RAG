## Background

Aisha Johnson is a Senior Backend Engineer at CyberLLM and a core contributor to NetLLM's log
aggregation pipeline and real-time correlation engine. Before joining CyberLLM, she spent five
years at Amazon Web Services, where she worked on the CloudWatch Logs team building distributed
ingestion pipelines capable of processing petabytes of log data per day. Her work at AWS focused
on fault-tolerant streaming architectures using Kafka, Kinesis, and custom sharding strategies.
She was promoted twice at AWS and received two "Bar Raiser" recognitions for her technical
interview contributions.

Prior to AWS, Aisha spent two years at a Texas-based managed security services provider (MSSP),
where she first encountered the challenge of correlating security events across heterogeneous
log sources. She holds a BS in Computer Engineering from the University of Texas at Austin and
an MS in Computer Science from Georgia Tech (OMSCS program, completed while at AWS). Her
graduate research focused on distributed stream processing for time-series anomaly detection.

Aisha was one of CyberLLM's first ten engineering hires, recruited by David Okonkwo after a
referral from a mutual UT Austin alumni connection. She architected NetLLM's log ingestion
layer from the ground up, designing the schema-on-read pipeline that allows customers to
forward logs from 200+ source types without pre-configuration. Her correlation engine — which
uses sliding-window pattern matching combined with Raj Patel's ML anomaly models — is
considered NetLLM's core technical differentiator. She leads a sub-team of three engineers
and is a go-to technical reviewer for all backend architecture decisions across CyberLLM.

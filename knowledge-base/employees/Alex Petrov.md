## Background

Alex Petrov is a Senior Threat Intelligence Analyst at CyberLLM, specializing in nation-state
threat actor tracking and indicator of compromise (IOC) correlation. Before joining CyberLLM,
he spent five years in the FBI's Cyber Division based in Washington, D.C., serving as a special
agent focused on cyber intrusion investigations involving state-sponsored threat groups. His
casework involved investigating intrusions attributed to APT28 (Fancy Bear), APT41 (Winnti),
and Lazarus Group. His analyses contributed to multiple federal indictments against foreign
nationals accused of conducting cyber espionage and intellectual property theft targeting U.S.
critical infrastructure, defense contractors, and financial institutions.

Prior to the FBI, Alex worked as a malware analyst at FireEye (now Mandiant) for two years,
where he reverse-engineered malware samples from APT campaigns and contributed to several
high-profile threat reports covering supply chain compromise and watering hole attacks. He holds
a BS in Computer Science from the University of Colorado Boulder and an MS in Digital Forensics
from Champlain College. He is a GIAC Certified Threat Intelligence Analyst (GCTI), GIAC
Certified Incident Handler (GCIH), and Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH). His combination of
federal law enforcement investigation skills and deep technical malware analysis expertise gives
him a unique bridge between intelligence, operations, and technology.

Alex was recruited by Priya Raghavan, who had collaborated with him on inter-agency threat
intelligence sharing during her NSA tenure. At CyberLLM, Alex tracks nation-state actors,
maintains behavioral profiles on 300+ APT groups, and curates IOC feeds powering ThreatLLM's
correlation engine. He designed the "ThreatScore" algorithm that ranks indicators by confidence,
freshness, and contextual relevance — a feature enterprise customers consistently cite as
ThreatLLM's key differentiator. He works remotely from Denver and travels to Austin quarterly.

## Background

Sophia Laurent is the Marketing Manager at CyberLLM, leading the company's demand generation,
content marketing, brand strategy, and event programs. Before CyberLLM, she spent five years at
Proofpoint as a Senior Marketing Lead, where she managed integrated campaigns for Proofpoint's
email security and threat protection product lines. At Proofpoint, she built a content engine that
produced 120+ technical blog posts, 30 webinars, and 15 whitepapers annually, contributing to
pipeline generation of $40M+ per year. She also co-led Proofpoint's flagship "Protect" conference,
managing a $2M event budget and 3,000+ attendees.

Prior to Proofpoint, Sophia worked in marketing at Rapid7 for two years, focusing on product launch
campaigns for InsightIDR and InsightVM. She earned her BA in Communications from NYU and an MBA
from Cornell Tech, where she focused on technology marketing and customer analytics. Her career has
been defined by her ability to translate complex cybersecurity capabilities into accessible
messaging that resonates with both technical practitioners and C-suite decision-makers.

Sophia was introduced to CyberLLM through Jordan Williams, whom she knew from a cybersecurity
marketing community. Impressed by the company's AI-native approach and the chance to build a
marketing function at a high-growth startup, she joined in March 2021. At CyberLLM, Sophia built
the marketing team from a one-person operation to a three-person function (herself, a content
specialist, and a design contractor). She launched CyberLLM's blog (averaging 15K monthly visits
by 2024), manages the social media presence, runs paid acquisition and ABM campaigns, and
conceived and launched "CyberDefend" — CyberLLM's annual customer and prospect conference that
debuted in 2023 with 200 attendees and doubled to 400 in 2024.

## Performance History

- **2021:** Rating: Exceeds Expectations. Built CyberLLM's marketing infrastructure from scratch —
  website redesign, HubSpot implementation, blog launch, and brand guidelines. Created messaging
  frameworks for all eight products. Generated $2.5M in marketing-sourced pipeline. Launched first
  three case studies and established CyberLLM's presence at Black Hat and RSA Conference.
- **2022:** Rating: Exceeds Expectations. Marketing-sourced pipeline grew to $6M. Launched ABM
  program targeting Fortune 500 CISOs in financial services and healthcare, aligning closely with
  Sarah Mitchell's sales strategy. Published 40+ blog posts and 8 whitepapers. Hired content
  specialist. CyberLLM's brand awareness score in target accounts increased from 12% to 28%.
- **2023:** Rating: Meets Expectations. Launched inaugural CyberDefend conference (200 attendees,
  $800K pipeline generated). Pipeline growth slowed alongside broader enterprise budget freeze.
  Pivoted to lower-funnel demand gen with ROI-focused messaging. Blog traffic grew to 10K/month.
  Conference logistics proved challenging as a one-person event lead — recommended dedicated event
  support for 2024.
- **2024:** Rating: Exceeds Expectations. CyberDefend 2024 doubled to 400 attendees and generated
  $2.1M in pipeline. Marketing-sourced pipeline reached $10M for the year. Launched CyberLLM's
  podcast "Defend Forward" (25 episodes, 8K monthly downloads). Led product launch campaigns for
  CompliLLM's NIST/DORA modules and ThreatScore v2.0 with coordinated PR, generating 12 press
  mentions including Dark Reading and SC Magazine.

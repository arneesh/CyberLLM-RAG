## Background

Raj Patel is the Senior Data Scientist at CyberLLM, leading the development of machine learning
models that power threat detection, anomaly scoring, and behavioral authentication across the
entire product portfolio. He holds a PhD in Statistical Learning from Stanford University, where
his dissertation focused on online learning algorithms for high-dimensional streaming data —
work directly applicable to real-time cybersecurity event analysis. During his PhD, he interned
at Google Brain, contributing to research on adversarial robustness of deep learning classifiers,
and published six peer-reviewed papers in NeurIPS, ICML, and KDD.

Before CyberLLM, Raj spent two years as a research scientist at Cylance (acquired by
BlackBerry), where he built ML models for next-generation antivirus file classification. His
gradient-boosted ensemble model achieved a 99.2% detection rate with a 0.1% false positive
rate on the Cylance benchmark dataset, and he co-authored three patents on ML-based malware
classification. His combination of rigorous statistical foundations and applied cybersecurity ML
experience is rare in the industry.

Raj was recruited by Marcus Chen, who had read his KDD paper on streaming anomaly detection
and recognized its applicability to CyberLLM's product vision. At CyberLLM, Raj has built the
ML models embedded across multiple products: the behavioral endpoint detection models in
ShieldLLM, the network traffic anomaly scorer in NetLLM, the user behavior analytics engine
in IdentLLM, and the vulnerability severity predictor in PenLLM. He collaborates closely with
Alex Petrov on threat intelligence-informed model features and with Aisha Johnson on model
serving infrastructure within the log pipeline. He manages a small ML team of two engineers.

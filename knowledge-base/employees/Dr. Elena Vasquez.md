## Background

Dr. Elena Vasquez is the technical visionary behind CyberLLM's AI-powered cybersecurity platform.
She earned her PhD in Machine Learning from MIT in 2011, where her dissertation on adversarial
robustness in deep neural networks was awarded the ACM Doctoral Dissertation Award Honorable
Mention. Her academic work focused on building ML systems that could withstand deliberate
manipulation — a prescient research direction that became central to modern cybersecurity. She
published 15 papers during her PhD, including two best-paper awards at NeurIPS and ICML.

After MIT, Elena joined Palo Alto Networks as a senior engineer and rapidly rose to principal
engineer, leading a team of 20 researchers building next-generation threat detection models. She
architected Palo Alto's first ML-based malware classification system, which reduced false positive
rates by 60% across their enterprise customer base. During her five years there, she filed 12
patents related to neural network architectures for real-time network analysis and contributed to
open-source security tooling that remains widely adopted in the industry. Her reputation as one of
the top ML security researchers in the world made her a frequent keynote speaker at academic and
industry conferences.

Elena connected with Marcus Chen through the academic conference circuit and their shared belief
that large language models could fundamentally transform cybersecurity operations. She left Palo
Alto Networks in late 2017 to co-found CyberLLM. As CTO, she designed the unified AI engine that
powers all eight CyberLLM products, from ThreatLLM's threat intelligence correlation to IdentLLM's
behavioral authentication models. She leads a team of 12 engineers and researchers and remains
CyberLLM's most prolific technical contributor, personally reviewing every major architectural
decision and conducting weekly design reviews with all engineering squads.

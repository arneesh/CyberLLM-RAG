## Background

Yuki Tanaka is a Mobile Developer at CyberLLM, responsible for building and maintaining the
company's mobile security dashboard application used by SOC analysts and security leaders. Before
CyberLLM, she spent four years at Line Corporation in Tokyo as a Senior Mobile Engineer, where
she worked on Line's messaging platform serving 180+ million monthly active users. At Line, she
contributed to the iOS and Android clients, built the end-to-end encryption implementation for
Line's "Letter Sealing" feature, and led a team of three mobile engineers who delivered the
in-app payment security module processing $2B+ in annual transactions. Her experience building
secure, high-performance mobile applications at massive scale provided a strong technical
foundation for CyberLLM's security-focused mobile work.

Yuki grew up in Osaka, Japan, and earned her BS in Information Science from Kyoto University.
She moved to San Francisco in 2021, drawn by the U.S. startup ecosystem and a desire to work at
the intersection of mobile development and cybersecurity. She is fluent in Japanese and English,
holds an AWS Certified Developer – Associate certification, and is proficient in Swift, Kotlin,
React Native, and Flutter. Her experience with cross-platform mobile frameworks has been
instrumental in CyberLLM's ability to ship iOS and Android applications with a single developer.

Yuki discovered CyberLLM through a React Native meetup in San Francisco, where Derek Chang
presented on building security dashboards. After learning about CyberLLM's plans for a mobile
application, she reached out to the engineering team. At CyberLLM, Yuki is the sole mobile
developer, owning the CyberLLM Mobile app — a cross-platform application (React Native) that
provides SOC analysts with real-time alert notifications, incident response workflows, threat
intelligence summaries, and executive security dashboards accessible from anywhere. The app
integrates with NetLLM, ThreatLLM, IncidentLLM, and ShieldLLM APIs and supports biometric
authentication, certificate pinning, and offline caching for use in air-gapped environments.

## Background

Grace Kim is the UX/UI Designer at CyberLLM, responsible for the user experience and visual
design across all eight products. Her most significant contribution is CyberLLM's unified
security dashboard — a single pane of glass that aggregates alerts, threat intelligence,
compliance status, and incident timelines from ThreatLLM, ShieldLLM, NetLLM, VaultLLM,
CompliLLM, IncidentLLM, PenLLM, and IdentLLM. Before joining CyberLLM, she spent two and a
half years on Figma's design team, where she worked on the collaborative editing features and
component library system used by millions of designers worldwide. Her Figma experience gave her
deep expertise in design systems, interactive prototyping, and developer handoff workflows.

Prior to Figma, Grace worked as a junior product designer at a San Francisco-based healthcare
SaaS startup for one year, where she first learned to design for data-dense, mission-critical
interfaces — a skill directly transferable to cybersecurity dashboards. She holds a BFA in
Interaction Design from the California College of the Arts and completed the Nielsen Norman
Group UX certification program. She is proficient in Figma, Framer, and has working knowledge
of React and Tailwind CSS, enabling her to prototype interactive components directly in code.

Grace was recruited after CyberLLM realized its product UX was becoming a competitive liability.
Customer feedback surveys in early 2021 showed a satisfaction gap between product capability
(rated highly) and usability (rated average). Grace was hired to unify the fragmented product
interfaces into a cohesive design system. She created "CyberLLM Design Language" (CDL) — a
comprehensive component library, typography scale, color system, and interaction pattern guide
that all engineering teams now use. She works remotely from San Francisco and visits Austin
bimonthly for design sprints.

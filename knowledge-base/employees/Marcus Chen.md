## Background

Marcus Chen co-founded CyberLLM in January 2018 after a distinguished career split between national
defense research and academia. He spent six years as a program manager and researcher at DARPA,
where he led initiatives in adversarial machine learning and autonomous cyber defense systems. His
work on Project CHESS (Cyber Hardened Environments through Synthetic Simulation) earned recognition
from the Department of Defense and laid the groundwork for what would become CyberLLM's core threat
modeling approach. At DARPA, he also oversaw $35 million in research grants distributed across
university labs and defense contractors, sharpening the program management skills he would later
bring to running a company.

Following his DARPA tenure, Marcus joined the University of Texas at Austin as an assistant
professor of computer science, eventually earning tenure in the cybersecurity and AI research group.
He published over 40 peer-reviewed papers on adversarial networks, intrusion detection, and
reinforcement learning applied to network defense. His graduate students consistently placed at top
industry labs including Google Brain, DeepMind, and OpenAI. It was during this period that he and
Elena Vasquez began collaborating on the foundational research that would become CyberLLM. Their
joint paper, "Large Language Models as Cyber Threat Reasoning Engines," published at USENIX Security
2017, became the intellectual foundation for the company's product suite and has been cited over 600
times.

Marcus made the leap from academia to entrepreneurship when he secured a $4.2 million seed round led
by Andreessen Horowitz in late 2017. Known for his ability to translate complex AI research into
commercial products, he has grown CyberLLM from a two-person startup to a 35-employee company with
eight product lines generating $38 million in annual recurring revenue. He remains deeply involved
in product strategy and serves as the public face of the company at major industry conferences like
Black Hat, RSA, and Gartner Security & Risk Management Summit. He sits on the advisory boards of the
Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) and the Austin Technology Council.

## Performance History

- **2018:** Rating: Exceptional. Successfully launched CyberLLM and incorporated in Texas. Secured
$4.2M seed funding from Andreessen Horowitz. Recruited founding engineering team of 5. Established
partnership with UT Austin for ongoing research collaboration. Set company mission, values, and
initial product roadmap covering threat intelligence and endpoint detection.

- **2019:** Rating: Exceptional. Closed Series A at $18M led by Sequoia Capital. Launched
ThreatLLM and ShieldLLM as first commercial products, generating $900K ARR in the first year. Grew
company to 15 employees. Named to Austin Business Journal's "40 Under 40." Recruited David Okonkwo
as CISO and Priya Raghavan to lead threat research.

- **2020:** Rating: Exceeds Expectations. Navigated company through COVID-19 pandemic with zero
layoffs and transitioned to remote-first operations within two weeks. Launched NetLLM and
VaultLLM. Revenue grew 340% year-over-year to $3.2M ARR. Brought on critical hires in sales (Sarah
Mitchell), engineering (Mei-Lin Zhang), and offensive security (Carlos Mendez, Alex Petrov).

- **2021:** Rating: Exceeds Expectations. Closed Series B at $52M led by Accel Partners. Expanded
product suite to include CompliLLM and IncidentLLM. Company reached 28 employees. Established New
York and San Francisco satellite offices. ARR grew to $11M. Board noted strong execution but
encouraged Marcus to delegate more operational responsibilities.

- **2022:** Rating: Exceptional. Revenue crossed $20M ARR, exceeding board targets by 15%.
Launched PenLLM and IdentLLM, completing the 8-product portfolio. Won "Cybersecurity Startup of
the Year" at SC Awards. Keynoted RSA Conference to an audience of 3,000. Company reached
profitability on a unit-economics basis.

- **2023:** Rating: Meets Expectations. Growth slowed to 45% amid macro headwinds and enterprise
budget freezes. Pipeline generation declined in Q2 and Q3. Board expressed concern about burn
rate; Marcus implemented cost controls reducing OpEx by 18% while preserving R&D investment. ARR
reached $29M but missed the $32M board target. Marcus acknowledged the need for improved
forecasting discipline.

- **2024:** Rating: Exceeds Expectations. Revenue rebounded to $38M ARR, exceeding the $35M
target. Signed three Fortune 100 customers in financial services and healthcare. Successfully led
Series C discussions at a $280M pre-money valuation. Company reached 35 employees with 92% annual
retention. Launched first annual customer conference "CyberLLM Defend" with 400 attendees.

## HR Notes

- Marcus maintains an open-door policy and holds weekly all-hands meetings every Friday at 4:00 PM
CT. Employee engagement surveys consistently cite his transparency and accessibility as a top
cultural strength. He personally onboards every new hire during their first week, conducting a
90-minute "CyberLLM 101" session covering company history, mission, and product strategy. This
practice has been maintained even as the company has grown to 35 employees, and it is one of the
most positively cited onboarding experiences in exit interviews.

- He retains his adjunct faculty appointment at UT Austin and mentors two PhD students whose
research feeds into CyberLLM's product roadmap. CyberLLM benefits from this through a research
pipeline and early access to top graduate talent. However, his academic commitments occasionally
create scheduling conflicts during product launch cycles, which was flagged in his 2022
performance review. He has since limited his university time to one day per week during Q4 (peak
launch season).

- In 2023, HR flagged a concern about work-life balance after Marcus logged consecutive 80-hour
weeks during Series C preparation. He agreed to take a two-week sabbatical in January 2024 and has
since adopted a more sustainable schedule, delegating more operational decisions to Dr. Vasquez
and Ryan Nakamura. His 2024 engagement survey self-assessment reflected improved satisfaction with
work-life balance, though he remains CyberLLM's most frequent after-hours Slack user.

## Background

David Okonkwo is CyberLLM's Chief Information Security Officer, responsible for the company's
internal security posture, regulatory compliance, and serving as a senior advisor to product
engineering teams on real-world threat landscapes. Before joining CyberLLM, David spent 11 years
at JPMorgan Chase, rising from senior security analyst to CISO of the firm's asset management
division. In that role, he oversaw a security program protecting $2.5 trillion in client assets,
managed a team of 45 security professionals across New York and London, and led the organization
through three SOC 2 Type II audits, multiple OCC regulatory examinations, and two major incident
response engagements involving nation-state threat actors. He received JPMorgan's "Risk Excellence
Award" in 2017 for his work on the firm's zero-trust network migration.

Prior to JPMorgan, David served as an information security officer at Lockheed Martin for five
years, working on classified defense programs requiring Top Secret/SCI clearance. His
responsibilities included securing satellite communications systems and conducting security
assessments for programs under the Defense Information Systems Agency (DISA). He holds a BS in
Information Systems from Howard University and an MS in Cybersecurity from Georgia Tech. He
maintains CISSP, CISM, and CRISC certifications and serves on the advisory board for the SANS
Institute's CISO Executive Education program.

Marcus Chen and Elena Vasquez recruited David specifically because they wanted a CISO who could
both protect CyberLLM as a company and serve as an internal "customer zero" for the product
suite. David deploys every CyberLLM product internally before it ships to customers, providing
detailed feedback on usability, detection efficacy, and operational workflows. His feedback has
been directly credited with over 50 product feature improvements. He frequently joins enterprise
sales calls to share how CyberLLM uses its own tools — a practice Sarah Mitchell calls "our most
powerful sales asset" — and his credibility as a former Fortune 500 CISO has been instrumental
in closing several major deals.

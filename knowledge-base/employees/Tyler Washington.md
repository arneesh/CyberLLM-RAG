## Background

Tyler Washington is a Junior Security Analyst at CyberLLM and the youngest member of the team.
He graduated from the University of Texas at Austin in May 2023 with a BS in Cybersecurity, where
he was a member of the ISSS (Information and Systems Security Society) and competed in the
National Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition (NCCDC), placing third regionally in his senior year.
His capstone project involved developing a machine learning classifier for phishing email detection
using transformer-based NLP models, which caught the attention of CyberLLM's engineering team
during a UT Austin career fair presentation.

Tyler grew up in Houston, Texas, and was drawn to cybersecurity after his family's small business
was hit by a ransomware attack during his freshman year of college. He interned at a managed
security service provider (MSSP) during the summers of 2021 and 2022, where he gained hands-on
experience with SIEM tools, incident triage, and vulnerability scanning. He holds a CompTIA
Security+ certification and is studying for his CySA+ while at CyberLLM.

At CyberLLM, Tyler participates in a structured rotation program designed by Amanda Cross, spending
three-month rotations through the SOC, threat research, and penetration testing teams. His first
rotation in the SOC involved monitoring NetLLM alerts and triaging incidents under the supervision
of senior analysts. He struggled initially with alert fatigue and time management — his first
90-day review flagged slow triage response times and difficulty prioritizing critical versus
informational alerts. After a structured improvement plan including daily standups, paired triage
sessions, and workload coaching, his performance improved significantly in the second half of his
SOC rotation. He is currently on his threat research rotation, contributing to ThreatLLM's IOC
curation under Alex Petrov's mentorship.

## Background

Fatima Al-Rashid is a Senior Data Engineer at CyberLLM, responsible for architecting and
maintaining the data pipelines that feed the company's machine learning models and analytics
platforms. Before CyberLLM, she spent five years at Palantir Technologies as a Forward Deployed
Engineer and later Data Engineering Lead, where she designed data integration platforms for defense
and intelligence community clients. Her work at Palantir involved building petabyte-scale data
pipelines processing signals intelligence, network telemetry, and open-source intelligence (OSINT)
feeds in near-real-time. She led a team of four engineers that delivered a streaming analytics
platform for a Five Eyes partner agency, processing 2 billion events per day with sub-second
latency.

Prior to Palantir, Fatima worked as a data engineer at Amazon Web Services for three years on the
Kinesis team, contributing to the streaming data service's internal tooling and customer
onboarding pipelines. She earned her BS in Computer Engineering from the University of Michigan
and her MS in Data Science from Georgia Tech (OMSCS program, completed while at AWS). She is an
Apache Spark committer and has presented at Kafka Summit and Strata Data Conference.

Fatima was recruited by Marcus Chen through a mutual connection in the Austin tech community. At
CyberLLM, she owns the end-to-end data infrastructure — from raw log ingestion and normalization
to feature engineering pipelines that feed ThreatLLM's correlation models, ShieldLLM's behavioral
detection engine, and NetLLM's anomaly detection algorithms. She designed CyberLLM's unified data
lake on AWS (S3 + Iceberg + Spark), the Kafka-based streaming pipeline processing 500M+ events
per day from customer deployments, and the feature store that enables ML engineers to rapidly
experiment with new detection models. Her infrastructure is considered the backbone of CyberLLM's
AI capabilities.

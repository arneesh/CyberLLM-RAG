## Background

Derek Chang is a Full Stack Developer at CyberLLM, responsible for building and maintaining
customer-facing web interfaces across several CyberLLM products. Before joining CyberLLM, he
spent four years at Okta as a Senior Software Engineer on the Admin Console team, where he built
identity management dashboards, policy configuration workflows, and SSO integration wizards used
by over 15,000 enterprise customers. His work at Okta focused on React frontends backed by Node.js
and Go microservices, and he developed deep expertise in building intuitive UIs for complex
security and identity management use cases. He shipped Okta's multi-factor authentication
enrollment flow redesign, which improved adoption rates by 22%.

Derek earned his BS in Computer Science from UC Berkeley, where he was a teaching assistant for
the introductory web development course. Before Okta, he worked for two years at a YC-backed SaaS
startup building a project management tool, gaining experience with the full startup lifecycle from
MVP through Series A. His identity and access management background at Okta made him a natural fit
for CyberLLM's IdentLLM product.

Derek was recruited by CyberLLM's CTO after discovering a job posting on Hacker News. At CyberLLM,
he is the primary developer behind IdentLLM's admin portal — a web application that allows
enterprise security teams to configure zero-trust access policies, manage identity federation, and
monitor authentication anomalies. He also contributes to VaultLLM's key management interface,
building the UI for encryption key lifecycle management (generation, rotation, revocation, and
audit trails). Derek works closely with the product team to translate complex security concepts
into accessible interfaces and is known for his strong design instincts and meticulous attention
to frontend performance and accessibility.

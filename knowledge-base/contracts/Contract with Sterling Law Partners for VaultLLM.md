# Data Protection Services Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. and Sterling Law Partners LLP — VaultLLM Professional

**Document ID:** CL-VLM-2024-0588  
**Effective Date:** August 1, 2024  
**Prepared By:** CyberLLM Legal Vertical Sales

---

## 1. Parties

This Data Protection Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

- **CyberLLM Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with principal offices at 440 Cyber Ridge Boulevard, Austin, TX 78701 ("Provider")
- **Sterling Law Partners LLP**, a limited liability partnership organized under the laws of New York, with offices at 600 Lexington Avenue, 30th Floor, New York, NY 10022 ("Client")

## 2. Term and Effective Date

- **Effective Date:** August 1, 2024
- **Initial Term:** Twenty-four (24) months, concluding July 31, 2026
- **Contract Value (Initial Term):** $132,000

## 3. Scope of Services

Provider shall deliver the **VaultLLM Professional** platform configured for legal industry data protection requirements, including:

- End-to-end encryption for all client matter files, case documents, and attorney work product at rest and in transit using AES-256 encryption
- Attorney-client privilege data classification engine that automatically identifies, tags, and applies heightened protection policies to privileged communications
- Integration with Client's e-discovery platform (Relativity) for seamless encrypted document review workflows without exposing decrypted content outside the review environment
- American Bar Association (ABA) Model Rules of Professional Conduct compliance monitoring, specifically Rules 1.6 (Confidentiality) and 1.1 (Competence in technology)
- Ethical wall enforcement with cryptographic access controls to prevent conflicts of interest across practice groups
- Encrypted secure file sharing portal for Client's external communications with opposing counsel, courts, and expert witnesses
- Data loss prevention (DLP) policies tailored for legal document types (pleadings, contracts, memoranda, correspondence)
- Quarterly compliance audit reports aligned with Client's professional liability insurance requirements

## 4. Fees and Payment Terms

| Fee Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Monthly Platform Fee | $5,500 |
| One-Time Deployment & Integration Fee | $12,000 |

- Invoices are issued monthly on the first business day and are payable within **thirty (30) days**.
- Late payments shall accrue interest at **1.5% per month** on the overdue amount.
- The deployment fee covers Relativity integration, ethical wall configuration, initial privilege classification model training, and a one-day on-site training workshop for Client's attorneys and paralegals.

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

- **Platform Uptime:** 99.9% measured monthly
- **Encryption Service Availability:** 99.95% — reflecting the critical nature of real-time encryption/decryption for active legal matters
- **Critical Incident Response:** Within thirty (30) minutes, 24/7/365
- **High-Severity Response:** Within two (2) hours during business hours
- **Standard Response:** Within one (1) business day
- **SLA Credit:** 5% of the monthly fee for each 0.1% below the uptime commitment, capped at 25%.

## 6. Support

- **Tier:** Professional Support
- **Assigned Contact:** Named Technical Account Manager with legal technology experience
- **Support Channels:** Encrypted email, phone support during extended business hours (Mon–Fri, 7:00 AM – 8:00 PM ET), emergency hotline for after-hours critical issues
- **Training:** Initial deployment training included; ongoing CLE-eligible cybersecurity awareness sessions available quarterly at no additional charge
- **Quarterly Business Reviews:** Review of data protection posture, privilege classification accuracy metrics, and compliance status

## 7. Confidentiality

Provider acknowledges the extraordinary sensitivity of information processed under this Agreement, which may include attorney-client privileged communications, attorney work product, and personally identifiable information of Client's clientele. Provider shall implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards consistent with the ABA's guidance on technology and confidentiality. Provider's personnel with access to Client data shall execute individual confidentiality agreements and undergo background screening. Confidentiality obligations under this section are perpetual and shall survive termination of this Agreement indefinitely with respect to privileged materials.

## 8. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive **twelve (12) month** terms unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least **sixty (60) days** prior to the end of the then-current term. Renewal pricing shall not increase by more than **3%** per year above the prior term's monthly fee.

## 9. Termination

- Either party may terminate for material breach upon **forty-five (45) days** written notice, provided the breaching party fails to cure within that period.
- Client may terminate for convenience with **sixty (60) days** written notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to **three (3) months** of the monthly platform fee.
- Upon termination, Provider shall cooperate with Client's transition to an alternative solution and shall ensure all encrypted data is exported in a usable format within forty-five (45) days. Provider shall certify in writing the secure destruction of all copies of Client data within sixty (60) days of termination.

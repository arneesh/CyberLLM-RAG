# SaaS Subscription Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. and Premier Retail Group — IncidentLLM Platform

**Effective Date:** August 15, 2025
**Contract Number:** IL-PRG-2025-0102

---

## 1. Parties

- **Provider:** CyberLLM Inc., a Delaware corporation, with principal offices at 440 Security Boulevard, Suite 1200, Austin, TX 78701 ("CyberLLM").
- **Client:** Premier Retail Group, LLC, a Minnesota limited liability company operating 340 retail locations nationwide, with principal offices at 1200 Nicollet Mall, Minneapolis, MN 55403 ("Client").

## 2. Term and Effective Date

This Agreement shall commence on August 15, 2025 and continue for an initial term of twelve (12) months, expiring on August 14, 2026 (the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 8.

## 3. Subscription Tier and Monthly Fee

- **Tier:** IncidentLLM Core
- **Monthly Subscription Fee:** $4,500.00 USD
- **One-Time Setup Fee:** $7,500.00 USD (due upon execution)

## 4. Scope of Services and Features

CyberLLM shall provide Client access to the IncidentLLM Core platform, including:

- Point-of-sale (POS) breach response playbooks tailored to Client's NCR and Verifone terminal environments, covering card skimming detection, RAM scraping malware containment, and terminal isolation procedures.
- PCI-DSS incident handling workflows aligned to PCI Incident Response Guidance, including cardholder data compromise assessment, card brand notification procedures, and PFI (PCI Forensic Investigator) engagement coordination.
- Basic SOAR automation with 30+ retail-focused playbooks covering phishing response, ransomware containment, e-commerce platform compromise, and gift card fraud.
- Incident classification and prioritization engine calibrated for retail threat landscape (Magecart attacks, supply chain compromise, seasonal threat spikes).
- Integration with Client's existing Microsoft Sentinel SIEM via pre-built data connector.
- Automated incident notification to acquiring banks and payment card brands per PCI Operating Regulations.
- Post-incident reporting with root cause analysis templates and lessons-learned documentation.
- Compliance-ready incident logs suitable for PCI-DSS Requirement 12.10 audit evidence.

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

- **Platform Uptime:** CyberLLM guarantees 99.5% monthly uptime for the IncidentLLM Core platform.
- **Critical Incident Response:** Initial triage within one (1) hour during business hours (7:00 AM–9:00 PM CT, 7 days/week); within four (4) hours outside business hours.
- **Standard Incidents:** Response within one (1) business day.
- **SLA Credits:** For each full percentage point below 99.5% monthly uptime, Client receives a 5% credit on that month's fee, capped at 15%.

## 6. Payment Terms

- Monthly subscription fees are invoiced on the first business day of each calendar month.
- Payment is due within thirty (30) days of invoice date via ACH, check, or corporate credit card.
- Late payments shall incur a finance charge of 1.0% per month on the outstanding balance.
- The one-time setup fee is invoiced at contract execution and is non-refundable.

## 7. Support

- **Support Tier:** Standard Support (Core tier)
- **Channels:** Email and in-platform ticketing system. Phone support available during business hours.
- **Response Times:** Critical within 1 hour (business hours); high within 4 hours; standard within 1 business day.
- **Training:** One (1) remote onboarding session (up to 3 hours) for Client's security and IT operations team.
- **Knowledge Base:** Access to CyberLLM's self-service documentation portal and retail threat advisory library.
- **Seasonal Readiness:** CyberLLM shall provide a pre-holiday threat briefing and playbook review prior to the November–December retail peak season.

## 8. Termination

- Either party may terminate for cause upon thirty (30) days' written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within fifteen (15) days.
- Client may terminate for convenience upon thirty (30) days' written notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to two (2) months of subscription fees.
- During an active PCI-related incident investigation, neither party may terminate this Agreement until the investigation concludes or both parties mutually agree in writing.
- Upon termination, CyberLLM shall provide Client with all incident records and forensic data in exportable format within fifteen (15) days.

## 9. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days prior to the end of the then-current term. Renewal pricing may increase by up to 7% with forty-five (45) days' advance written notice.

## 10. Confidentiality

Each party shall maintain the confidentiality of the other's proprietary information. CyberLLM shall treat all cardholder data, POS system configurations, and store network architecture details as strictly confidential. CyberLLM shall not store cardholder data beyond what is necessary for incident investigation and shall purge such data within ninety (90) days of incident closure.

## 11. Limitation of Liability

Neither party's aggregate liability shall exceed the total fees paid or payable during the twelve (12) months preceding the claim. Neither party shall be liable for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages, except in cases of willful misconduct or breach of confidentiality obligations.

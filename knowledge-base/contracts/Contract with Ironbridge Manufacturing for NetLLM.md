# Professional Services and Subscription Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. and Ironbridge Manufacturing — NetLLM Professional (OT/IT Edition)

**Document ID:** CL-NLM-2025-0203  
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2025  
**Prepared By:** CyberLLM Industrial Solutions Division

---

## 1. Parties

This Professional Services and Subscription Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

- **CyberLLM Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with principal offices at 440 Cyber Ridge Boulevard, Austin, TX 78701 ("Provider")
- **Ironbridge Manufacturing**, a Pennsylvania corporation headquartered at 3100 Steel Valley Industrial Park, Pittsburgh, PA 15201 ("Client")

## 2. Term and Effective Date

- **Effective Date:** March 1, 2025
- **Initial Term:** Eighteen (18) months, concluding August 31, 2026
- **Contract Value (Initial Term):** $162,000

## 3. Scope of Services

Provider shall deliver the **NetLLM Professional** platform with specialized configuration for industrial manufacturing OT/IT convergence environments, including:

- Unified monitoring across Client's corporate IT network and operational technology (OT) environments spanning three (3) plant locations
- Purdue Model-aware network segmentation monitoring covering Levels 0 through 5, with automatic detection of unauthorized cross-level communications
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) alignment with automated controls mapping and gap analysis reporting
- Passive OT network discovery and asset inventory for SCADA systems, PLCs, HMIs, and industrial IoT sensors without disrupting production processes
- IT/OT convergence threat detection models trained on manufacturing-specific attack patterns (e.g., Triton, Industroyer variants)
- Integration with Client's existing Rockwell Automation and Siemens industrial control platforms via OPC-UA read-only connectors
- Bi-weekly threat intelligence briefings focused on the manufacturing and critical infrastructure sectors
- Quarterly NIST CSF maturity assessment reports with remediation recommendations

## 4. Fees and Payment Terms

| Fee Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Monthly Platform Fee | $9,000 |
| One-Time OT Assessment & Deployment Fee | $20,000 |

- All invoices are issued on the first business day of each calendar month and are payable within **thirty (30) days** of the invoice date.
- Late payments shall incur a finance charge of **1.5% per month** on the unpaid balance.
- The one-time deployment fee covers the initial OT network assessment, sensor placement, Purdue Model mapping, and ICS integration services, invoiced in two equal installments: 50% upon execution and 50% upon completion of deployment.

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

- **Platform Uptime:** 99.9% measured monthly
- **OT Critical Alert Response:** Within fifteen (15) minutes, 24/7/365 — any alert indicating potential impact to physical production systems
- **IT Critical Alert Response:** Within thirty (30) minutes, 24/7/365
- **High-Severity Response:** Within two (2) hours
- **Standard Response:** Within eight (8) business hours
- **SLA Credit:** For each 0.1% below 99.9% uptime, Client receives a 5% credit on that month's fee, capped at 25%.

## 6. Support

- **Tier:** Professional Support with OT Specialization
- **Named Contact:** Assigned Technical Account Manager with industrial cybersecurity certification (GICSP or equivalent)
- **Support Channels:** Dedicated phone line, encrypted email, secure portal with OT-specific knowledge base
- **On-Site Support:** Up to two (2) on-site visits per year included for sensor maintenance, tuning, and ICS integration review
- **Quarterly Business Reviews:** Joint review sessions including both IT security and plant operations stakeholders

## 7. Confidentiality

Both parties recognize that performance under this Agreement involves access to highly sensitive information, including Client's manufacturing processes, industrial control system configurations, plant floor network topologies, and production schedules. Provider agrees to restrict access to such information to personnel holding appropriate background checks and who have executed individual confidentiality agreements. Confidentiality obligations shall survive for five (5) years following the termination of this Agreement, reflecting the long operational lifecycle of industrial control systems.

## 8. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive **twelve (12) month** periods unless either party delivers written notice of non-renewal at least **sixty (60) days** prior to the end of the then-current term. Annual pricing adjustments on renewal shall not exceed **4%** above the prior period's monthly fee.

## 9. Termination

- Either party may terminate for material breach upon **forty-five (45) days** written notice if the breach remains uncured within that period.
- Client may terminate for convenience upon **ninety (90) days** written notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to **four (4) months** of the monthly platform fee.
- In the event of termination, Provider shall conduct an orderly decommissioning of all OT sensors and monitoring agents within sixty (60) days, at no additional charge to Client.
- Provider shall return or securely destroy all Client data, including OT asset inventories and network maps, within forty-five (45) days of termination.

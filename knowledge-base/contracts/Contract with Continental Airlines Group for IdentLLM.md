# Master Service Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. & Continental Airlines Group — IdentLLM Enterprise

**Agreement Number:** CL-ILM-2024-0055
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Document Classification:** Confidential

---

## 1. Parties

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

- **CyberLLM Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with principal offices at 440 Cipher Boulevard, Austin, TX 78701 ("Provider")
- **Continental Airlines Group**, a Texas corporation with principal offices at 1600 Skyway Center Drive, Houston, TX 77032 ("Client")

## 2. Term and Duration

This Agreement shall commence on January 1, 2024, and shall continue for a period of **thirty-six (36) months**, terminating on December 31, 2026, unless renewed or terminated in accordance with the provisions herein.

## 3. Product and Scope of Services

Provider shall deliver **IdentLLM Enterprise** — an AI-powered identity and access management and Zero Trust platform — configured for a large-scale airline operations environment supporting approximately 45,000 employees. The scope includes:

- Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA) architecture deployment across Client's corporate, airport operations, and maintenance facility networks, enforcing continuous identity verification and least-privilege access
- Single Sign-On (SSO) federation across Client's crew scheduling system (CrewTrac), flight operations platform (FlightDeck Pro), reservation systems, and corporate applications — supporting SAML 2.0, OIDC, and WS-Federation protocols
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) rollout for all 45,000 employees, with adaptive risk-based authentication leveraging CyberLLM's behavioral AI engine, supporting hardware tokens for cockpit crew and biometric options for ground staff
- Privileged Access Management (PAM) for aircraft maintenance systems, engine diagnostic platforms, and safety-critical IT infrastructure, with session recording and just-in-time access provisioning
- Automated identity lifecycle management with HR system integration (Workday) for onboarding, role changes, and offboarding across union and non-union employee populations
- Real-time identity threat detection monitoring for credential stuffing, impossible travel, and anomalous access patterns across Client's global operations
- Compliance reporting dashboards aligned with TSA cybersecurity directives, FAA regulations, and SOX requirements

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Provider guarantees the following service levels:

- **Platform Uptime:** 99.99% measured monthly for authentication services, excluding scheduled maintenance windows (maintenance limited to 2 hours/month during off-peak hours, 2 AM–4 AM CT)
- **Authentication Latency:** SSO and MFA transactions completed within 300 milliseconds at the 99th percentile
- **Identity Provisioning:** Automated onboarding completed within 15 minutes of HR trigger event
- **Identity Threat Alert Response:** Critical alerts triaged and escalated within 10 minutes
- **SLA Credits:** For each 0.01% below the uptime guarantee, Client shall receive a credit of 5% of that month's fee, up to a maximum of 30%

## 6. Support

Client shall receive **Tier 1 — Dedicated Enterprise Support**, which includes:

- 24/7/365 access to CyberLLM's Identity Operations Center (IOC) via phone, encrypted Slack channel, and dedicated support portal
- Named Identity Architect and Customer Success Manager assigned to the Continental Airlines account
- Weekly operational sync calls with Client's IT and Security leadership during the deployment phase; monthly thereafter
- Escalation to CyberLLM's VP of Engineering within 1 hour for authentication platform outages
- On-site support at Client's Houston headquarters and major hub airports available upon request (up to 12 visits per year, travel expenses billed separately)

## 7. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to maintain the confidentiality of all proprietary information exchanged under this Agreement. Confidential information includes, but is not limited to, employee identity data, access control policies, network architecture details, crew scheduling information, and financial terms. Provider shall implement additional safeguards for Sensitive Security Information (SSI) as defined by 49 CFR Part 1520. This obligation survives termination for a period of five (5) years.

## 8. Data Handling and Compliance

- All identity data shall be encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Provider shall maintain SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, and FedRAMP Moderate certifications
- Provider shall support Client's compliance with TSA cybersecurity directives (SD 1580-21-01 series), SOX Section 404 IT controls, and PCI-DSS requirements for payment-handling systems
- Provider shall ensure data residency within the continental United States for all identity and access management data
- Annual penetration testing results for the IdentLLM platform shall be made available to Client upon request

## 9. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twenty-four (24) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least one hundred twenty (120) days prior to the end of the then-current term. Renewal pricing shall be subject to negotiation, with increases not to exceed 5% per annum absent mutual written agreement.

## 10. Termination

- Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon sixty (60) days' written notice if the other party materially breaches any provision and fails to cure such breach within the notice period.
- Client may terminate for convenience with one hundred eighty (180) days' written notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to 50% of the remaining monthly fees for the unexpired term.
- Provider shall provide a transition assistance period of up to ninety (90) days following termination to support Client's migration to an alternative platform, billed at Provider's then-current professional services rates.
- Upon termination, Provider shall securely destroy or return all Client identity data within thirty (30) days and provide written certification of destruction.

## 11. Limitation of Liability

Provider's aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by Client in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim. Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages. This limitation shall not apply to breaches of confidentiality obligations, data protection failures, or willful misconduct.

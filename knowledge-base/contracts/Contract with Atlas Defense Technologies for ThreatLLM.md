# Master Service Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. & Atlas Defense Technologies — ThreatLLM Enterprise (Classified)

**Agreement Number:** CL-TLM-2024-0203
**Effective Date:** May 1, 2024
**Document Classification:** CUI // NOFORN
**Contract Vehicle:** Direct Commercial Agreement

---

## 1. Parties

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

- **CyberLLM Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with principal offices at 440 Cipher Boulevard, Austin, TX 78701, CAGE Code: 8X7K2 ("Provider")
- **Atlas Defense Technologies**, a Virginia corporation with principal offices at 7800 Patriot Gateway, Arlington, VA 22203, CAGE Code: 3M9R1 ("Client")

## 2. Term and Duration

This Agreement shall be effective from May 1, 2024, and shall continue for a period of **forty-eight (48) months**, terminating on April 30, 2028. This extended term reflects the complexity of classified environment deployment and the strategic nature of the engagement.

## 3. Product and Scope of Services

Provider shall deliver **ThreatLLM Enterprise** configured for defense and classified environments. The scope includes:

### 3.1 Core Threat Intelligence
- Nation-state threat actor tracking with emphasis on adversaries relevant to U.S. defense interests (APT groups attributed to China, Russia, Iran, North Korea, and emerging threat actors)
- Defense Industrial Base (DIB) specific threat feeds covering supply chain compromise, espionage campaigns, and insider threat indicators
- AI-powered analysis of adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) mapped to MITRE ATT&CK for Enterprise and ICS frameworks

### 3.2 Classified Environment Support
- Air-gapped deployment option with quarterly manual intelligence updates delivered via classified courier or approved cross-domain solution
- Support for deployment within Client's SCIF and classified network segments
- All Provider personnel supporting this contract shall hold active U.S. security clearances at the Secret level or above

### 3.3 Compliance and Accreditation
- FedRAMP High authorization for cloud-hosted components
- NIST SP 800-171 and CMMC Level 3 compliance for all CUI handling
- Support for Client's Authority to Operate (ATO) processes, including provision of security documentation and architecture diagrams
- DFARS 252.204-7012 compliance for cyber incident reporting

### 3.4 Advanced Capabilities
- Custom AI models trained on defense sector threat data, delivered as containerized appliances for on-premises deployment
- Threat hunting playbooks aligned to DIB-specific threat scenarios
- Integration with Client's existing Elastic Security SIEM and Tenable vulnerability management platforms
- Red team intelligence: curated adversary emulation plans based on real-world campaigns

## 4. Fees and Payment Terms

| Fee Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Monthly Platform Fee | $20,000.00 |
| One-Time Deployment & Accreditation Support Fee | $75,000.00 |
| Annual Air-Gapped Update Service | $30,000.00 |

- Monthly fees invoiced on the 1st of each month; net 45 days.
- Late payments accrue interest at 1.0% per month.
- Deployment fee invoiced in two installments: 50% upon contract execution, 50% upon successful initial deployment.
- Air-gapped update service billed annually on the contract anniversary date.

## 5. Service Level Agreement

- **Cloud Platform Uptime:** 99.99% monthly (applies to unclassified cloud components only)
- **Air-Gapped Intelligence Delivery:** Within 5 business days of scheduled quarterly update
- **Critical Threat Alert Notification:** Within 30 minutes of validation for unclassified feeds; within 24 hours of next scheduled update for air-gapped systems
- **SLA Credits:** 5% of monthly fee per 0.05% below uptime target, capped at 25%

## 6. Support

Client receives **Tier 1 — Dedicated Defense Support**:

- 24/7/365 support from cleared CyberLLM defense team personnel
- Named Technical Account Manager (TAM) and Threat Intelligence Liaison, both holding TS/SCI clearances
- Monthly classified threat briefings (in-person at Client facility or via approved secure communications)
- Priority escalation to CyberLLM's Advanced Threat Research group within 1 hour for critical incidents
- On-site support: up to 8 visits per year included; additional visits at $2,500/day plus travel

## 7. Security and Compliance

- Provider shall maintain a Facility Clearance (FCL) at the Secret level throughout the term.
- All data classified at CUI or above shall be handled in accordance with 32 CFR Part 2002.
- Provider shall submit to annual security audits by Client or Client-designated third party.
- Cyber incidents shall be reported to Client and to DC3/DIBCISS within 72 hours per DFARS requirements.

## 8. Confidentiality

Both parties shall protect all classified and proprietary information in accordance with applicable federal regulations, including the National Industrial Security Program (NISP). Unauthorized disclosure may result in criminal penalties. Unclassified confidential information shall be protected for a period of seven (7) years following termination.

## 9. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twenty-four (24) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least one hundred eighty (180) days prior to the end of the then-current term. Pricing adjustments for renewal terms shall be negotiated in good faith, with increases capped at 4% annually.

## 10. Termination

- **For Cause:** Either party may terminate upon ninety (90) days' written notice for material breach that remains uncured after the notice period.
- **For Convenience:** Client may terminate with one hundred eighty (180) days' notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to 40% of remaining monthly fees.
- **Government Action:** If Client's prime government contract is terminated or defunded, Client may terminate this Agreement with sixty (60) days' notice without early termination penalty.
- **Data Handling:** Upon termination, all classified data shall be destroyed per DoD 5220.22-M standards. Provider shall furnish a Certificate of Destruction within thirty (30) days.

## 11. Limitation of Liability

Provider's aggregate liability shall not exceed the total fees paid in the preceding twelve (12) months. This limitation does not apply to breaches of confidentiality involving classified information or willful misconduct.

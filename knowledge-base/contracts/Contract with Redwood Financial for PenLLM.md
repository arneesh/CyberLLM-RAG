# Master Service Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. & Redwood Financial — PenLLM Professional

**Agreement Number:** CL-PLM-2024-0147
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2024
**Document Classification:** Confidential

---

## 1. Parties

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

- **CyberLLM Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with principal offices at 440 Cipher Boulevard, Austin, TX 78701 ("Provider")
- **Redwood Financial**, a California state-chartered banking corporation with principal offices at 1200 Sequoia Drive, Sacramento, CA 95814 ("Client")

## 2. Term and Duration

This Agreement shall commence on March 1, 2024, and shall continue for a period of **twenty-four (24) months**, terminating on February 28, 2026, unless renewed or terminated in accordance with the provisions herein.

## 3. Product and Scope of Services

Provider shall deliver **PenLLM Professional** — an AI-driven vulnerability management and penetration testing platform — configured for regional banking environments. The scope includes:

- Continuous automated vulnerability scanning across all Client-owned external and internal network assets, including branch office subnets
- Quarterly AI-orchestrated penetration tests targeting Client's online banking portal, mobile applications, and internal teller systems
- PCI-DSS vulnerability management module with automated compliance gap reporting and remediation prioritization
- Integration with Client's existing Qualys asset inventory and Jira ticketing system for remediation workflow tracking
- Real-time vulnerability correlation engine mapping findings to the NIST NVD and CISA KEV catalogs
- Executive-level risk dashboards with trend analysis across quarterly assessment cycles
- Remediation SLAs: Critical vulnerabilities flagged within 1 hour, with guided remediation playbooks delivered within 4 hours

## 4. Fees and Payment Terms

| Fee Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Monthly Platform Fee | $7,000.00 |
| One-Time Setup & Integration Fee | $15,000.00 |
| Quarterly Pen Test Reports | Included |

- All fees are billed monthly in arrears. Invoices are issued on the 1st of each calendar month.
- Payment is due within thirty (30) days of invoice date.
- Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less.
- The one-time setup fee shall be invoiced upon execution of this Agreement and is due within thirty (30) days.

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Provider guarantees the following service levels:

- **Platform Uptime:** 99.9% measured monthly, excluding scheduled maintenance windows
- **Scan Completion:** Full external perimeter scan completed within 8 hours of initiation
- **Critical Vulnerability Notification:** Within 1 hour of validated discovery
- **SLA Credits:** For each 0.1% below the uptime guarantee, Client shall receive a credit of 5% of that month's fee, up to a maximum of 25%

## 6. Support

Client shall receive **Tier 2 — Professional Support**, which includes:

- 12/5 access (Monday–Friday, 7 AM–7 PM CT) to CyberLLM's Vulnerability Management team via phone, email, and support portal
- Named account manager with monthly check-in calls
- Priority response for critical severity findings: initial response within 2 hours
- Access to CyberLLM's remediation knowledge base and guided fix documentation
- Escalation to Senior Penetration Test Engineers within 4 hours for disputed or complex findings

## 7. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to maintain the confidentiality of all proprietary information exchanged under this Agreement. Confidential information includes, but is not limited to, vulnerability assessment results, penetration test reports, network architecture diagrams, and financial terms. This obligation survives termination for a period of five (5) years.

## 8. Data Handling and Compliance

- All vulnerability data and pen test findings shall be encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Provider shall maintain SOC 2 Type II certification and undergo annual third-party security audits
- Provider acknowledges Client's obligations under GLBA, PCI-DSS, and FFIEC examination guidelines and shall support compliance evidence gathering
- Penetration test reports shall be formatted to satisfy OCC and state banking regulator examination requirements

## 9. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current term. Renewal pricing shall not increase by more than 4% annually without mutual written agreement.

## 10. Termination

- Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon thirty (30) days' written notice if the other party materially breaches any provision and fails to cure such breach within the notice period.
- Client may terminate for convenience with ninety (90) days' written notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to 40% of the remaining monthly fees for the unexpired term.
- Upon termination, Provider shall securely destroy or return all Client vulnerability data and pen test artifacts within thirty (30) days and provide written certification of destruction.

## 11. Limitation of Liability

Provider's aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by Client in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim. Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages.

# Government Services Contract: CyberLLM Inc. and Federal Transit Authority — VaultLLM Enterprise (FedRAMP)

**Document ID:** CL-VLM-2024-GOV-0091  
**Effective Date:** October 1, 2024  
**Prepared By:** CyberLLM Public Sector Division

---

## 1. Parties

This Government Services Contract ("Contract") is entered into by and between:

- **CyberLLM Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with principal offices at 440 Cyber Ridge Boulevard, Austin, TX 78701 ("Contractor")
- **Federal Transit Authority**, a federal agency of the United States Department of Transportation, located at 1200 New Jersey Avenue SE, Washington, DC 20590 ("Agency")

## 2. Term and Effective Date

- **Effective Date:** October 1, 2024
- **Period of Performance:** Forty-eight (48) months (Base Period: 24 months, plus two 12-month option periods)
- **Conclusion of Base Period:** September 30, 2026
- **Conclusion with All Options Exercised:** September 30, 2028
- **Total Contract Ceiling (All Periods):** $480,000

## 3. Scope of Services

Contractor shall deliver the **VaultLLM Enterprise** platform in an on-premises deployment configuration meeting federal security requirements, including:

- FIPS 140-2 Level 3 validated encryption modules for all data-at-rest and data-in-transit protection across Agency systems
- FedRAMP Moderate authorization baseline compliance, with Contractor maintaining an active FedRAMP Moderate Authorization to Operate (ATO)
- On-premises deployment within Agency's FISMA-accredited data center located in the continental United States, with no data leaving U.S. sovereign territory
- Data residency controls ensuring all encryption keys, encrypted data, and audit logs are stored exclusively within Agency-controlled infrastructure
- Integration with Agency's existing identity and access management (IAM) infrastructure, including PIV/CAC smart card authentication
- Automated compliance reporting aligned with NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 controls and OMB Circular A-130
- Encrypted data classification and handling for Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) per NIST SP 800-171
- Quarterly Independent Verification & Validation (IV&V) assessments submitted to Agency's Authorizing Official

## 4. Fees and Payment Terms

| Fee Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Monthly Service Fee (Base Period) | $10,000 |
| Monthly Service Fee (Option Periods) | $10,000 |
| One-Time On-Premises Deployment Fee | $35,000 |

- Invoicing shall follow the Prompt Payment Act (31 U.S.C. § 3903). Invoices are submitted monthly through the Agency's designated invoicing system.
- Payment is due within **thirty (30) days** of receipt of a proper invoice.
- The one-time deployment fee covers hardware provisioning support, FIPS-validated module installation, PIV integration, and Authority to Operate documentation support.
- All pricing is firm-fixed-price for the base period. Option period pricing is subject to EPA adjustment per FAR 52.216-2.

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

- **System Availability:** 99.9% measured monthly during core operational hours (Mon–Fri, 6:00 AM – 10:00 PM ET)
- **Critical Incident Response:** Within thirty (30) minutes during core hours; within two (2) hours outside core hours
- **High-Severity Response:** Within two (2) hours during core hours
- **Standard Response:** Within one (1) business day
- **SLA Remedies:** Failure to meet availability targets for two (2) consecutive months shall entitle Agency to a 10% reduction in the following month's service fee and may be considered grounds for Contractor performance evaluation reporting.

## 6. Support

- **Tier:** Government Enterprise Support
- **Cleared Personnel:** All support staff with access to Agency systems shall hold or obtain appropriate Public Trust or higher clearances as required by Agency
- **Support Channels:** Government-approved secure communications (encrypted email, secure phone), ticketing through Agency's ServiceNow instance
- **On-Site Support:** Up to four (4) on-site visits per year to Agency's data center included at no additional charge
- **Training:** Annual security awareness training for up to fifty (50) Agency personnel included

## 7. Confidentiality and Security

Contractor acknowledges that all information processed, stored, or transmitted under this Contract may include sensitive federal data subject to statutory and regulatory protections. Contractor shall comply with all applicable federal information security requirements including FISMA, FedRAMP, NIST standards, and Agency-specific security policies. Contractor personnel shall sign Agency non-disclosure agreements prior to accessing any Agency system. Contractor shall report any suspected security incident to Agency's CISO within one (1) hour of detection. All Contractor obligations regarding federal data protection shall survive indefinitely following the expiration or termination of this Contract.

## 8. Option Periods and Renewal

- **Option Period 1:** October 1, 2026 through September 30, 2027 — exercisable by Agency with sixty (60) days written notice
- **Option Period 2:** October 1, 2027 through September 30, 2028 — exercisable by Agency with sixty (60) days written notice
- Exercise of option periods is at the sole discretion of the Agency. This Contract does not automatically renew; continuation beyond the base period requires affirmative exercise of options.

## 9. Termination

- **Termination for Convenience:** Agency may terminate this Contract at any time in accordance with FAR 52.249-2 (Termination for Convenience of the Government).
- **Termination for Cause:** Agency may terminate for default if Contractor fails to perform in accordance with Contract terms per FAR 52.249-8.
- **Transition Assistance:** Upon termination or expiration, Contractor shall provide transition assistance for up to ninety (90) days at no additional cost, including data migration, key transfer, and documentation handover.
- **Data Disposition:** All Agency data, encryption keys, and audit records shall be returned to Agency or securely destroyed per NIST SP 800-88 guidelines within thirty (30) days of Contract conclusion, with written certification of destruction provided to Agency's Contracting Officer.

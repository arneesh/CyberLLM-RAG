# SaaS Services Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. and GlobalBank Holdings — IncidentLLM Platform

**Effective Date:** May 15, 2025
**Contract Number:** IL-GBH-2025-0055

---

## 1. Parties

- **Provider:** CyberLLM Inc., a Delaware corporation, with principal offices at 440 Security Boulevard, Suite 1200, Austin, TX 78701 ("CyberLLM").
- **Client:** GlobalBank Holdings, Inc., a nationally chartered bank holding company regulated by the OCC and Federal Reserve, with principal offices at 200 Liberty Street, New York, NY 10281 ("Client").

## 2. Term and Effective Date

This Agreement shall commence on May 15, 2025 and continue for an initial term of twenty-four (24) months, expiring on May 14, 2027 (the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 8.

## 3. Subscription Tier and Monthly Fee

- **Tier:** IncidentLLM Advanced
- **Monthly Subscription Fee:** $10,000.00 USD
- **One-Time Setup and Integration Fee:** $20,000.00 USD (due upon execution)

## 4. Scope of Services and Features

CyberLLM shall provide Client access to the IncidentLLM Advanced platform, including:

- SWIFT Customer Security Programme (CSP) integration with automated monitoring of SWIFT Alliance Lite2 and Alliance Access environments for anomalous transaction patterns and unauthorized message modifications.
- Financial fraud incident response workflows covering account takeover, business email compromise (BEC), ACH/wire fraud, and card-not-present fraud scenarios.
- Regulatory notification automation generating pre-formatted Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) for FinCEN filing, OCC incident notifications, and Federal Reserve event reports within mandated timeframes.
- SOAR automation engine with 75+ financial-services-specific playbooks including ATM jackpotting response, core banking intrusion containment, and third-party vendor breach coordination.
- Integration with Client's existing Splunk SIEM, CrowdStrike EDR, and Palo Alto XSOAR environments via bi-directional API connectors.
- Incident timeline reconstruction engine with automated evidence preservation and chain-of-custody management for law enforcement referrals.
- Real-time threat intelligence enrichment from FS-ISAC (Financial Services Information Sharing and Analysis Center) feeds.
- Board-level incident reporting with customizable executive dashboards and post-incident review automation.

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

- **Platform Uptime:** CyberLLM guarantees 99.95% monthly uptime.
- **Critical Incident Response:** Initial triage response within thirty (30) minutes, 24/7/365.
- **High-Priority Incidents:** Initial response within two (2) hours.
- **SLA Credits:** For uptime breaches, Client receives 3% of the monthly fee per 0.01% below threshold, capped at 30%. Critical SLA breaches shall result in a $2,500 credit per occurrence.

## 6. Payment Terms

- Monthly subscription fees are invoiced on the first business day of each month.
- Payment is due within thirty (30) days of invoice date via wire transfer or ACH.
- Late payments shall accrue interest at 1.5% per month on the overdue balance.
- The one-time setup fee shall be invoiced at contract execution and is non-refundable.
- CyberLLM shall provide detailed invoices with project codes aligned to Client's internal cost-center allocation requirements.

## 7. Support

- **Support Tier:** Advanced Support (24/7)
- **Channels:** Dedicated phone line, encrypted email, in-platform ticketing, and emergency pager for critical incidents.
- **Response Times:** Critical within 30 minutes; high within 2 hours; medium within 4 hours; low within 1 business day.
- **Incident Response Retainer:** Client shall have access to CyberLLM's incident response team for up to two (2) major incident engagements per year (up to 40 person-hours each) at no additional charge.
- **Training:** Two (2) onboarding sessions and quarterly refresher webinars for Client's SOC team.

## 8. Termination

- Either party may terminate for cause upon sixty (60) days' written notice following a material breach that remains uncured for thirty (30) days.
- Client may terminate for convenience upon ninety (90) days' written notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to four (4) months of subscription fees.
- CyberLLM may not terminate for convenience during the Initial Term.
- Upon termination, CyberLLM shall provide all incident data, playbooks, and forensic artifacts in an exportable format within thirty (30) days.

## 9. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current term. Renewal pricing may be adjusted by up to 5% with ninety (90) days' advance written notice.

## 10. Confidentiality and Regulatory Compliance

All Client data, including non-public personal information (NPI) as defined under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA), shall be treated as Confidential Information. CyberLLM shall comply with FFIEC examination requirements, OCC Bulletin 2013-29 (Third-Party Relationships), and shall make available audit reports (SOC 2 Type II) and penetration testing results upon Client's reasonable request. CyberLLM consents to examination by Client's regulators as required.

## 11. Limitation of Liability

Neither party's aggregate liability shall exceed the greater of (a) the total fees paid during the eighteen (18) months preceding the claim or (b) $500,000 USD. This cap shall not apply to breaches of confidentiality, data security obligations, or regulatory penalties arising from CyberLLM's negligence.

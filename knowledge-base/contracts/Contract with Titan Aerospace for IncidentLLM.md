# Master SaaS Services Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. and Titan Aerospace — IncidentLLM Platform

**Effective Date:** February 1, 2025
**Contract Number:** IL-TAE-2025-0007
**Classification Handling:** CUI — Controlled Unclassified Information

---

## 1. Parties

- **Provider:** CyberLLM Inc., a Delaware corporation, with principal offices at 440 Security Boulevard, Suite 1200, Austin, TX 78701 ("CyberLLM").
- **Client:** Titan Aerospace, a Virginia corporation and U.S. Department of Defense contractor, with principal offices at 8200 Aerospace Parkway, Chantilly, VA 20151 ("Client").

## 2. Term and Effective Date

This Agreement shall commence on February 1, 2025 and continue for an initial term of thirty-six (36) months, expiring on January 31, 2028 (the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 8.

## 3. Subscription Tier and Monthly Fee

- **Tier:** IncidentLLM Enterprise (Custom — Defense Configuration)
- **Monthly Subscription Fee:** $25,000.00 USD
- **One-Time Implementation and Integration Fee:** $75,000.00 USD (invoiced in three installments)
- **ITAR Compliance Surcharge:** Included in monthly fee

## 4. Scope of Services and Features

CyberLLM shall provide Client access to the IncidentLLM Enterprise platform in a dedicated, ITAR-compliant deployment:

- ITAR-compliant incident handling workflows ensuring all technical data and defense articles are processed exclusively by U.S. persons within FedRAMP High-equivalent infrastructure.
- Integration with classified network (SIPR) boundary monitoring systems via one-way data diode feed for incident telemetry ingestion (Client-provided hardware; CyberLLM-provided software connector).
- Fifteen (15) minute SLA for critical incident initial triage response, staffed by CyberLLM's cleared Security Operations Center (SOC) analysts, 24/7/365.
- SOAR automation engine with over 200 pre-built playbooks covering advanced persistent threat (APT) response, supply chain compromise, insider threat, and weapons system network isolation.
- NIST SP 800-171 and CMMC Level 3 aligned incident categorization and reporting workflows.
- Automated DFARS 252.204-7012 cyber incident reporting with 72-hour notification workflow to the DoD Cyber Crime Center (DC3).
- Threat intelligence enrichment via integration with CISA, DISA IAVA feeds, and Client's commercial threat intelligence subscriptions.
- Digital forensics toolkit including memory capture orchestration, disk imaging workflow management, and chain-of-custody evidence tracking.
- Executive-level incident briefing generation with automated timeline reconstruction and impact assessment.

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

- **Platform Uptime:** 99.99% monthly uptime guarantee for the dedicated IncidentLLM instance.
- **Critical Incident Response:** Initial triage within fifteen (15) minutes, 24/7/365, by U.S.-person cleared analysts.
- **High-Priority Incidents:** Initial response within one (1) hour.
- **Platform Incidents:** CyberLLM platform issues shall receive response within thirty (30) minutes.
- **SLA Credits:** For critical incident SLA breaches, Client shall receive a credit of $5,000 per occurrence. For uptime breaches, 5% of monthly fee per 0.01% below threshold, capped at 40%.

## 6. Payment Terms

- Monthly subscription fees are invoiced on the first business day of each month and payable within forty-five (45) days via government-approved wire transfer or ACH.
- Implementation fees shall be invoiced in three equal installments: at execution, at 90-day milestone, and at go-live acceptance.
- Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.0% per month. CyberLLM acknowledges that Client's payment obligations may be subject to government funding appropriation cycles and agrees to reasonable accommodation.
- All fees are exclusive of applicable taxes.

## 7. Support

- **Support Tier:** Enterprise Defense Premium (24/7/365)
- **Channels:** Secure encrypted email, dedicated secure phone line, classified-capable ticketing system, and on-site support as required.
- **Response Times:** Critical within 15 minutes; high within 1 hour; medium within 4 hours; low within 1 business day.
- **Dedicated Resources:** Two (2) named Senior Incident Response Engineers and one (1) Technical Account Manager with active security clearances.
- **Training:** On-site training at Client facilities (up to 5 days per year), plus unlimited remote training for incident response teams.
- **Tabletop Exercises:** CyberLLM shall facilitate two (2) tabletop incident response exercises per year at no additional charge.

## 8. Termination

- Either party may terminate for cause upon ninety (90) days' written notice following a material breach that remains uncured for forty-five (45) days.
- Client may terminate for convenience upon one hundred twenty (120) days' written notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to the lesser of (a) six (6) months of subscription fees or (b) the remaining balance of the Initial Term.
- Upon termination, CyberLLM shall sanitize all Client data, including classified and CUI materials, in accordance with NIST SP 800-88 Guidelines for Media Sanitization and provide a certificate of destruction within thirty (30) days.

## 9. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twenty-four (24) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least one hundred twenty (120) days prior to expiration. Renewal pricing shall be negotiated in good faith, with annual escalation not to exceed 3%.

## 10. Confidentiality and Security

CyberLLM shall comply with ITAR (22 CFR Parts 120–130), DFARS cybersecurity requirements, and NIST SP 800-171 throughout the term. All personnel with access to Client data shall be U.S. persons with active security clearances at the appropriate level. CyberLLM shall maintain FedRAMP High authorization or equivalent for the dedicated infrastructure and shall undergo annual third-party penetration testing with results shared with Client.

## 11. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Virginia. Disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration administered by JAMS in Fairfax, Virginia, with arbitrators holding appropriate security clearances as needed.

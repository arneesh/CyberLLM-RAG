# Master SaaS Services Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. and CityGrid Infrastructure — IncidentLLM Platform

**Effective Date:** October 1, 2025
**Contract Number:** IL-CGI-2025-0119

---

## 1. Parties

- **Provider:** CyberLLM Inc., a Delaware corporation, with principal offices at 440 Security Boulevard, Suite 1200, Austin, TX 78701 ("CyberLLM").
- **Client:** CityGrid Infrastructure, Inc., a Colorado corporation providing smart city technology and critical infrastructure management services to municipalities, with principal offices at 1600 Wynkoop Street, Suite 800, Denver, CO 80202 ("Client").

## 2. Term and Effective Date

This Agreement shall commence on October 1, 2025 and continue for an initial term of forty-eight (48) months, expiring on September 30, 2029 (the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 8. The extended term reflects the strategic nature of this engagement and the time required for full integration across Client's multi-city deployments.

## 3. Subscription Tier and Monthly Fee

- **Tier:** IncidentLLM Enterprise (Custom — Critical Infrastructure Configuration)
- **Monthly Subscription Fee:** $18,000.00 USD
- **One-Time Implementation Fee:** $50,000.00 USD (invoiced in four quarterly installments)
- **Licensed Deployments:** Up to twelve (12) metropolitan area deployments

## 4. Scope of Services and Features

CyberLLM shall provide Client access to the IncidentLLM Enterprise platform with critical infrastructure and IoT-specific capabilities:

- IoT incident response playbooks covering smart traffic systems, connected streetlight networks, environmental sensor arrays, smart water/wastewater management, and public safety communication systems.
- Critical infrastructure protection workflows aligned to NIST Cybersecurity Framework, CISA Cross-Sector Cybersecurity Performance Goals (CPGs), and sector-specific guidance for Government Facilities and Water/Wastewater Systems.
- Multi-agency coordination playbooks enabling structured incident communication and joint response with municipal police departments, fire departments, emergency management agencies, FBI field offices, and CISA regional advisors.
- IoT device fleet compromise assessment tools supporting bulk analysis of firmware integrity, anomalous device behavior, and network segmentation validation across tens of thousands of connected devices.
- Automated incident escalation to the Multi-State Information Sharing and Analysis Center (MS-ISAC) and relevant sector-specific ISACs.
- SOAR automation engine with 100+ playbooks customized for smart city threat scenarios including ransomware targeting municipal systems, DDoS against public-facing services, and physical-cyber convergence incidents.
- GIS-integrated incident visualization providing geospatial mapping of affected infrastructure, blast radius estimation, and resource allocation optimization.
- Citizen impact assessment engine estimating service disruption scope and generating public communication templates for municipal partners.
- Integration with Client's existing Elastic SIEM, Azure IoT Hub, and municipal 911 CAD (Computer-Aided Dispatch) systems.

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

- **Platform Uptime:** CyberLLM guarantees 99.95% monthly uptime for the dedicated IncidentLLM instance.
- **Critical Infrastructure Incidents:** Initial triage within fifteen (15) minutes, 24/7/365, for incidents affecting public safety or essential municipal services.
- **High-Priority Incidents:** Response within one (1) hour.
- **Standard Incidents:** Response within four (4) hours.
- **SLA Credits:** For critical infrastructure SLA breaches, $4,000 credit per occurrence. For uptime, 3% of monthly fee per 0.01% below threshold, capped at 30%.

## 6. Payment Terms

- Monthly subscription fees are invoiced on the first business day of each month.
- Payment is due within forty-five (45) days of invoice date via ACH or wire transfer. CyberLLM acknowledges that some payments may flow through municipal procurement processes and agrees to accommodate standard government payment timelines.
- Late payments shall accrue interest at 1.0% per month, subject to applicable government prompt-payment regulations.
- Implementation fee installments are invoiced quarterly over the first year and are non-refundable for completed milestones.

## 7. Support

- **Support Tier:** Enterprise Critical Infrastructure (24/7/365)
- **Channels:** Dedicated secure phone line, encrypted email, in-platform ticketing, and emergency SMS/pager system for public-safety-critical incidents.
- **Response Times:** Critical within 15 minutes; high within 1 hour; medium within 4 hours; low within 1 business day.
- **Dedicated Resources:** One (1) named Senior IoT Security Engineer, one (1) Critical Infrastructure Incident Commander, and one (1) Technical Account Manager.
- **Training:** On-site training at up to four (4) metropolitan deployments per year, plus monthly remote training webinars.
- **Exercises:** CyberLLM shall facilitate three (3) multi-agency tabletop exercises per year, including one involving a simulated physical-cyber convergence scenario.

## 8. Termination

- Either party may terminate for cause upon ninety (90) days' written notice following a material breach that remains uncured for sixty (60) days.
- Client may terminate for convenience upon one hundred eighty (180) days' written notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to six (6) months of subscription fees. The extended notice period reflects the critical nature of the services.
- CyberLLM shall maintain service continuity during any termination transition period and shall cooperate with Client's designated successor provider.
- Upon termination, CyberLLM shall transfer all incident data, playbook configurations, and integration specifications within sixty (60) days.

## 9. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twenty-four (24) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least one hundred twenty (120) days prior to the end of the then-current term. Renewal pricing shall be negotiated in good faith, with annual escalation capped at 4%.

## 10. Confidentiality and Critical Infrastructure Information

All Client data—including municipal network architectures, IoT device inventories, GIS data, and incident records—constitutes Critical Infrastructure Information (CII) protected under the Critical Infrastructure Information Act of 2002 (6 U.S.C. § 131). CyberLLM shall restrict access to such data to personnel with completed background checks and shall store all data in FedRAMP-authorized, U.S.-based infrastructure. CyberLLM shall cooperate with CISA and law enforcement as directed by Client in the event of a nationally significant cyber incident.

## 11. Limitation of Liability

Neither party's aggregate liability shall exceed the total fees paid or payable during the twenty-four (24) months preceding the claim. Liability arising from breaches of CII confidentiality, willful misconduct, or incidents resulting in physical harm to persons shall not be subject to this cap.

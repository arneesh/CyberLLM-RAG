# Master Service Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. & Oasis Hospitality International — IdentLLM Professional

**Agreement Number:** CL-ILM-2024-0178
**Effective Date:** May 1, 2024
**Document Classification:** Confidential

---

## 1. Parties

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

- **CyberLLM Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with principal offices at 440 Cipher Boulevard, Austin, TX 78701 ("Provider")
- **Oasis Hospitality International**, a Florida corporation with principal offices at 3200 Palm Promenade, Miami, FL 33131 ("Client")

## 2. Term and Duration

This Agreement shall commence on May 1, 2024, and shall continue for a period of **twenty-four (24) months**, terminating on April 30, 2026, unless renewed or terminated in accordance with the provisions herein.

## 3. Product and Scope of Services

Provider shall deliver **IdentLLM Professional** — an AI-powered identity and access management platform — configured for a distributed hospitality environment spanning approximately 8,000 employees across 120 hotel properties. The scope includes:

- Single Sign-On (SSO) federation enabling unified authentication across Client's property management system (Opera PMS), revenue management platform, loyalty program portal, and corporate applications — supporting SAML 2.0 and OIDC protocols
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) deployment for all 8,000 employees, with flexible authentication methods including push notifications, SMS OTP, and FIDO2 hardware keys for property general managers
- Seasonal workforce onboarding and offboarding automation with rules-based provisioning triggered by Client's UKG Workforce Central HR system, supporting rapid credential issuance and revocation for seasonal hires (estimated 3,000+ seasonal employees per cycle)
- Role-based access control (RBAC) templates tailored to hospitality roles: front desk, housekeeping, food & beverage, property management, and corporate staff
- Self-service password reset and account recovery portal branded with Oasis Hospitality visual identity, available in English, Spanish, and French
- Identity analytics dashboard tracking login patterns, failed authentication trends, and dormant account risks across all 120 properties

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Provider guarantees the following service levels:

- **Platform Uptime:** 99.9% measured monthly, excluding scheduled maintenance windows (maintenance scheduled during 2 AM–5 AM ET to minimize impact on hotel front desk operations)
- **Authentication Latency:** SSO and MFA transactions completed within 500 milliseconds at the 95th percentile
- **Seasonal Onboarding Provisioning:** New seasonal employee accounts provisioned within 30 minutes of HR system trigger
- **Offboarding Revocation:** Access revoked within 15 minutes of termination event in HR system
- **SLA Credits:** For each 0.1% below the uptime guarantee, Client shall receive a credit of 5% of that month's fee, up to a maximum of 25%

## 6. Support

Client shall receive **Tier 2 — Professional Support**, which includes:

- 16/7 support (6 AM–10 PM ET, 7 days/week) via phone, email, and support portal to cover hotel operations hours
- Named Customer Success Manager with bi-monthly check-in calls
- Priority response for authentication outages: initial response within 1 hour
- Standard issue response: within 4 business hours
- Access to CyberLLM's identity management knowledge base and property deployment guides
- Escalation to Senior Identity Engineers within 2 hours for multi-property authentication failures

## 7. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to maintain the confidentiality of all proprietary information exchanged under this Agreement. Confidential information includes, but is not limited to, employee identity data, guest-adjacent system configurations, property network details, HR system integrations, and financial terms. This obligation survives termination for a period of four (4) years.

## 8. Data Handling and Compliance

- All identity data shall be encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Provider shall maintain SOC 2 Type II certification
- Provider shall support Client's compliance with PCI-DSS requirements for systems handling payment card data and GDPR requirements for European properties
- Provider shall ensure that identity data for employees at EU-based properties is processed in compliance with EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework requirements
- Employee personally identifiable information (PII) shall not be used for any purpose beyond identity and access management services

## 9. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current term. Renewal pricing shall not increase by more than 4% annually without mutual written agreement.

## 10. Termination

- Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon thirty (30) days' written notice if the other party materially breaches any provision and fails to cure such breach within the notice period.
- Client may terminate for convenience with ninety (90) days' written notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to 40% of the remaining monthly fees for the unexpired term.
- Upon termination, Provider shall cooperate with Client's migration to an alternative identity platform for a period of up to sixty (60) days at no additional charge.
- Provider shall securely destroy or return all Client identity data within thirty (30) days of migration completion and provide written certification of destruction.

## 11. Limitation of Liability

Provider's aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by Client in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim. Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages.

# Master Service Agreement: CyberLLM Inc. & Patriot Defense Systems — PenLLM Enterprise

**Agreement Number:** CL-PLM-2024-0088
**Effective Date:** February 1, 2024
**Document Classification:** CUI // NOFORN

---

## 1. Parties

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

- **CyberLLM Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with principal offices at 440 Cipher Boulevard, Austin, TX 78701 ("Provider")
- **Patriot Defense Systems**, a Virginia corporation with principal offices at 900 Liberty Ridge Drive, McLean, VA 22102 ("Client")

## 2. Term and Duration

This Agreement shall commence on February 1, 2024, and shall continue for a period of **twenty-four (24) months**, terminating on January 31, 2026, unless renewed or terminated in accordance with the provisions herein.

## 3. Product and Scope of Services

Provider shall deliver **PenLLM Enterprise** — an AI-driven vulnerability management and penetration testing platform — with a custom air-gapped deployment tailored for defense industrial base environments. The scope includes:

- DISA Security Technical Implementation Guide (STIG) compliance scanning across Client's Windows, Linux, and network device assets, with automated STIG checklist generation for RMF package submissions
- AI-orchestrated penetration testing within classified (up to SECRET) and unclassified environments, conducted by CyberLLM personnel holding active TS/SCI clearances
- Air-gapped deployment architecture with no external network dependencies; all threat intelligence updates delivered via encrypted removable media following Client's data transfer protocols
- Vulnerability correlation against the NIST NVD, CISA KEV catalog, and DoD-specific vulnerability databases
- Automated Plan of Action and Milestones (POA&M) generation aligned with NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 and CMMC Level 3 control families
- Red team exercise support (2 engagements per year) simulating advanced persistent threats relevant to the defense sector
- Secure reporting portal hosted within Client's enclave, with role-based access control and audit logging

## 5. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Provider guarantees the following service levels:

- **Platform Availability:** 99.9% measured monthly within the air-gapped enclave (uptime measured by Client's monitoring systems)
- **STIG Scan Completion:** Full enterprise scan completed within 12 hours of initiation
- **Critical Vulnerability Notification:** Within 2 hours of validated discovery, delivered via the secure reporting portal
- **Penetration Test Report Delivery:** Final report delivered within 10 business days of engagement completion
- **SLA Credits:** For each 0.1% below the availability guarantee, Client shall receive a credit of 5% of that month's fee, up to a maximum of 20%

## 6. Support

Client shall receive **Tier 1 — Dedicated Enterprise Support (Cleared Personnel)**, which includes:

- Cleared support team (TS/SCI) available Monday–Friday, 7 AM–7 PM ET, with after-hours emergency escalation
- Named Program Manager and Technical Lead assigned to the Patriot Defense account
- Monthly program review meetings aligned with Client's RMF assessment timeline
- Escalation to CyberLLM's Director of Federal Programs within 4 hours for mission-critical issues
- On-site support at Client's classified facilities available upon request (up to 8 visits per year, inclusive of travel)

## 7. Confidentiality and Security

Both parties agree to maintain the confidentiality of all proprietary and controlled information exchanged under this Agreement. All information shall be handled in accordance with NIST SP 800-171 Rev. 2 and DFARS 252.204-7012 requirements for Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI). Classified information shall be handled pursuant to the National Industrial Security Program Operating Manual (NISPOM). This obligation survives termination indefinitely for classified information and for five (5) years for all other confidential information.

## 8. Data Handling and Compliance

- All vulnerability data shall be encrypted at rest (AES-256, FIPS 140-2 validated modules) and handled within Client's approved classified or CUI enclaves
- Provider shall maintain CMMC Level 3 certification and FedRAMP High authorization (or equivalent)
- Provider shall comply with ITAR, EAR, and all applicable export control regulations
- Annual security assessments of Provider's cleared facilities shall be made available to Client's Facility Security Officer upon request

## 9. Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current term. Renewal pricing adjustments shall be negotiated in good faith and shall not exceed the GSA Schedule escalation rate for the applicable SIN category.

## 10. Termination

- Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon sixty (60) days' written notice if the other party materially breaches any provision and fails to cure such breach within the notice period.
- Client may terminate for convenience with ninety (90) days' written notice, subject to an early termination fee equal to 35% of the remaining monthly fees for the unexpired term.
- Termination for U.S. Government contract flow-down requirements (e.g., loss of prime contract) shall not incur early termination fees.
- Upon termination, Provider shall securely destroy all Client data in accordance with DoD 5220.22-M standards and provide written certification of destruction within fifteen (15) days.

## 11. Limitation of Liability

Provider's aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by Client in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim. Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages. This limitation shall not apply to breaches of security obligations, confidentiality, or violations of applicable law.

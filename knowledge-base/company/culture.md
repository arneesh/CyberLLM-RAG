# CyberLLM Culture

## Our Vision

**A world where every organization has AI-powered defense.**

We believe that state-of-the-art cybersecurity should not be a privilege reserved for Fortune 100 companies with seven-figure security budgets. Every hospital, school district, municipal government, and mid-market business deserves protection that can keep pace with the threats they face. CyberLLM exists to close that gap.

## Our Mission

**Democratize enterprise-grade cybersecurity through intelligent automation.**

We build AI systems that augment human security teams, automate the tedious and repetitive, surface the critical and time-sensitive, and ultimately make world-class cyber defense accessible to organizations of every size. Our products are designed so that a three-person IT team can operate with the confidence and coverage of a 50-person SOC.

## Core Values

### 1. Security-First Mindset

We practice what we preach. Security is not an afterthought at CyberLLM — it is the foundation of everything we build, ship, and operate. Every engineer is expected to think like an attacker. Every product decision is evaluated through a threat model. We run continuous internal red team exercises, maintain a bug bounty program, and publish our own security audit results. If we wouldn't trust our products to protect CyberLLM, we don't ship them to customers.

### 2. Radical Transparency

We operate with open books and open doors. Financial performance, product roadmaps, customer feedback, incident post-mortems, and strategic decisions are shared company-wide in our weekly All Hands meetings. We believe that informed team members make better decisions. Externally, we publish transparency reports, disclose vulnerabilities responsibly, and communicate honestly with customers — even when the news is not what they want to hear.

### 3. Relentless Innovation

The threat landscape changes daily. Our products must change faster. We allocate 20% of engineering capacity to exploratory R&D. Every quarter, we run a Hack Week where the entire company — not just engineers — can pitch, prototype, and present new ideas. Some of our most successful features, including our AI-powered phishing detection engine and our automated compliance evidence collector, started as Hack Week projects. We file patents, publish research, and contribute to open-source security tools because advancing the field benefits everyone.

### 4. Collective Resilience

Cybersecurity is a team sport, and so is working at CyberLLM. We build redundancy into our systems and our teams. No single person is a bottleneck. Knowledge is documented and shared. When incidents happen — and they do — we respond as a unit, debrief without blame, and emerge stronger. We support each other through the high-pressure moments that come with defending critical infrastructure, and we celebrate wins together.

## How We Work

### Remote-First, Intentionally Connected

CyberLLM is a remote-friendly company with 35 employees distributed across four offices (Austin, NYC, SF, London) and several fully remote locations. We default to asynchronous communication via Slack and Notion, with synchronous time reserved for high-bandwidth collaboration. Our core hours overlap window is 10 AM – 2 PM Central Time, ensuring that every team member — regardless of time zone — has at least four hours of shared availability.

We gather the full company in Austin twice per year for our "CyberSprint" offsite: a week of strategic planning, team building, hands-on workshops, and social events. These gatherings are where relationships are forged and the next quarter's biggest bets are shaped.

### Flat Hierarchy, High Autonomy

With 35 people, we maintain a deliberately flat organizational structure. There are no more than two layers of management between any individual contributor and the CEO. Team leads are player-coaches who spend the majority of their time contributing directly. Decisions are pushed to the edges — the people closest to the problem have the authority and the responsibility to solve it.

### Engineering Culture

Our engineering team follows a trunk-based development model with continuous deployment. Code reviews are mandatory, and every pull request requires at least one approval from a team member outside the submitter's immediate squad. We invest heavily in automated testing (unit, integration, end-to-end, and chaos engineering), and our deployment pipeline includes security scanning at every stage (SAST, DAST, SCA, container scanning). Mean time to production for a typical change is under four hours.

### Security Research Culture

Our security research team maintains active threat intelligence operations, including dark web monitoring and honeypot networks. Researchers are encouraged to present at industry conferences (Black Hat, DEF CON, RSA, BSides), publish papers, and contribute to open-source projects. CyberLLM sponsors employee participation in Capture the Flag (CTF) competitions and maintains an internal CTF platform for skill development and team bonding.

## Traditions and Rituals

- **Hack Week** — Quarterly, company-wide. One full week dedicated to experimentation and prototyping. Teams form organically around ideas, and winning projects receive funding to ship to production.
- **CTF Fridays** — Monthly internal CTF challenges designed by the security research team. Open to all employees, not just engineers. Winners earn bragging rights and CyberLLM swag.
- **Threat Briefings** — Biweekly 30-minute sessions where the security research team presents the latest threat landscape developments to the full company.
- **CyberSprint** — Biannual all-company offsite in Austin. Strategic planning, workshops, team building, and social events.
- **Open Source Fridays** — Once per month, engineers are encouraged to contribute to open-source security projects during working hours.
- **Lunch & Learn** — Weekly presentations by team members on topics ranging from advanced persistent threat groups to Rust memory safety to sales objection handling.

## Benefits and Perks

CyberLLM offers a comprehensive benefits package designed for a lean, high-performing team:

- **Unlimited PTO** — We trust our team to manage their own time. Average usage is 25 days per year, and we actively encourage at least 15 days off.
- **Home Office Stipend** — $2,500 annual budget for ergonomic furniture, monitors, peripherals, and other home office essentials.
- **Conference & Learning Budget** — $3,000 per year per employee for conferences, courses, certifications, and books. Black Hat and DEF CON attendance is strongly encouraged for all technical staff.
- **Equity** — Every employee receives stock options as part of their compensation package, vesting over four years with a one-year cliff.
- **Health Insurance** — 100% employer-paid premiums for employees; 75% for dependents. Medical, dental, and vision through Cigna.
- **401(k) Match** — 4% employer match, fully vested from day one.
- **Parental Leave** — 16 weeks fully paid for all new parents, regardless of gender or path to parenthood.
- **Mental Health** — Free access to Headspace, Talkspace, and a quarterly wellness stipend of $250.
- **Hardware** — Choice of MacBook Pro or high-spec Linux workstation, refreshed every two years.
- **Internet Stipend** — $100/month toward home internet costs.

## Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion

CyberLLM is committed to building a team that reflects the diversity of the world we protect. We partner with organizations like Women in Cybersecurity (WiCyS), the International Consortium of Minority Cybersecurity Professionals (ICMCP), and VetSecOps to expand our talent pipeline. Our hiring process uses structured interviews with standardized rubrics to minimize bias. We track and publish demographic data annually and hold ourselves accountable to measurable DEI goals.

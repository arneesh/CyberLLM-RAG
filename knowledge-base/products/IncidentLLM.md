# IncidentLLM

## Incident Response & SOAR Platform

IncidentLLM is CyberLLM's security orchestration, automation, and response (SOAR) platform, purpose-built to help security teams manage the full lifecycle of a cyber incident — from initial detection through triage, investigation, containment, eradication, recovery, and post-incident review. In environments where analysts face hundreds of alerts per day and mean time to respond (MTTR) is the difference between a contained event and a catastrophic breach, IncidentLLM's AI-driven automation ensures that the right actions happen at machine speed.

The platform serves as the central nervous system of a security operations center (SOC), integrating with detection tools (ThreatLLM, ShieldLLM, NetLLM, third-party SIEMs), identity systems (IdentLLM, Okta, Azure AD), IT service management platforms (ServiceNow, Jira), and communication tools (Slack, Teams, PagerDuty) to orchestrate coordinated, cross-tool response actions through customizable playbooks. IncidentLLM's AI triage engine automatically enriches, deduplicates, and prioritizes incoming alerts, reducing alert fatigue by up to 85% and freeing analysts to focus on genuine threats.

When a significant incident occurs, IncidentLLM provides a real-time war room — a collaborative workspace where analysts, incident commanders, and executives can coordinate response activities, share findings, track action items, and maintain a living timeline of the event. After the incident is resolved, the platform automatically generates comprehensive post-incident reports that satisfy regulatory notification requirements, support legal and insurance proceedings, and drive lessons-learned improvements.

## Key Features

- **Automated Incident Triage** — AI-powered triage engine ingests alerts from all connected detection sources, enriches them with threat intelligence from ThreatLLM, deduplicates related alerts into unified incidents, assigns severity scores, and routes incidents to the appropriate response team — all within seconds of detection.
- **Playbook Orchestration** — Visual playbook builder with 200+ prebuilt response playbooks for common incident types (ransomware, phishing, data exfiltration, insider threat, DDoS, account compromise). Playbooks orchestrate actions across 80+ integrated tools, supporting fully automated, semi-automated (human-in-the-loop), and manual execution modes.
- **Cross-Tool Integration** — Bidirectional integrations with 80+ security, IT, and communication tools. Actions include isolating endpoints (ShieldLLM, CrowdStrike), blocking IPs (firewalls, WAFs), disabling accounts (Okta, Azure AD), creating tickets (ServiceNow, Jira), and sending notifications (Slack, Teams, PagerDuty, email).
- **Timeline Reconstruction** — Automatically builds a chronological, evidence-backed timeline of incident events by correlating data from endpoint telemetry, network logs, identity events, and threat intelligence. Timelines are interactive, searchable, and exportable for forensic and legal use.
- **Post-Incident Reporting** — AI-generated incident reports that include executive summary, technical details, timeline, impact assessment, root cause analysis, containment actions taken, remediation status, and recommendations. Reports are formatted for regulatory notifications (GDPR 72-hour, HIPAA, SEC), board briefings, and cyber insurance claims.
- **War Room Collaboration** — Real-time collaborative workspace for active incidents. Includes shared timeline, task assignments, evidence uploads, chat, video conferencing bridge, and stakeholder status updates. Supports role-based access for analysts, incident commanders, legal, PR, and executive leadership.
- **Metrics and Analytics** — Comprehensive incident metrics including MTTD (mean time to detect), MTTR (mean time to respond), MTTC (mean time to contain), incident volume trends, playbook execution rates, and analyst workload distribution. Dashboards are customizable and exportable.
- **Runbook Automation** — Beyond security incidents, IncidentLLM supports IT operational runbooks for routine tasks like certificate renewals, patching workflows, and access reviews, reducing operational toil for security teams that also handle IT operations.
